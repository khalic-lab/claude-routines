## 🚀 New models, datasets, & open weights

- **GLM-5.2 open weights go live on Hugging Face under MIT.** `[vendor PR]` Zhipu AI (operating
  internationally as **Z.ai**) published the weights for **GLM-5.2**, its new flagship, on the Hub
  today. The model card confirms firsthand: a **Mixture-of-Experts** model (MoE — only a small
  subset of the network's "expert" sub-networks fire per token, so a huge total parameter count
  stays cheap to run) of roughly **744–753 billion total parameters**, architecture tagged
  `glm_moe_dsa`, **MIT licence**, bilingual English/Chinese, with live inference already wired up on
  Z.ai's own endpoint and on Novita. ([HF model card, 16 Jun 2026](https://hf.co/zai-org/GLM-5.2))
  Per secondary coverage, the design runs **~40B active parameters per token across 384 experts**,
  uses **DeepSeek Sparse Attention** (the same sparse-attention trick that lets DeepSeek's models
  stretch context cheaply) to reach a **1-million-token context window** (up from 200K in GLM-5.1;
  ~750,000 words held at once), and was trained on **28.5 trillion tokens**.
  ([Pandaily, Jun 2026](https://pandaily.com/zhipu-ai-glm-5-dot-2-open-source-mit-jun2026) `[via snippet]`;
  triangulated by [CometAPI](https://www.cometapi.com/what-is-glm-5-2/) `[via snippet]`) The model
  was first announced ~13 June on the GLM Coding Plan tiers; today's Hub publication is the
  open-weights step — the part that matters for anyone wanting to self-host. Why it matters: it puts
  a frontier-scale, genuinely open (MIT, not a restricted "open-ish" licence) coding model into
  anyone's hands at the exact moment access to the strongest *closed* Western models is being
  curtailed for non-US users (see Industry). It is also the single largest open-weight drop of the
  week.

- **No accompanying GGUF/MLX day-one ports yet in window.** The usual community quantisers
  (`unsloth`, `mradermacher`, mlx-community) had not posted GLM-5.2 ports to the Hub by the time of
  writing — unsurprising for a ~744B-param model that needs serious disk and conversion time.
  Earlier-week ports of **MiniMax M3** and **Kimi K2.7-Code** were already covered 12–13 June and are

## 💼 Industry, funding, regulation

- **The US has ordered Anthropic to cut off all foreign access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 — and
  Anthropic complied by disabling them globally.** This is the week's defining industry story and,
  as far as our briefs go, uncovered until now. Anthropic says it received a Commerce Department
  export-control letter at **5:21pm on Friday** instructing it to block **all foreign nationals** —
  including foreign staff working *inside* the US and foreign nationals physically in the country —
  from its two newest frontier models, **Claude Fable 5** and **Claude Mythos 5**, citing
  national-security concerns the letter did not spell out in detail.
  ([Al Jazeera, 13 Jun 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/13/us-orders-anthropic-to-disable-ai-models-for-all-foreign-nationals)
  `[via snippet]`) Because policing access by nationality alone is impractical, Anthropic responded
  by **disabling the two models for everyone, worldwide**, to guarantee compliance.
  ([Fortune, 13 Jun 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/06/13/anthropic-disables-fable-mythos-export-controls-national-security-threat/)
  `[via snippet]`; [Bloomberg, 13 Jun 2026](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-13/anthropic-says-us-limits-foreign-access-to-fable-5-mythos-5)
  `[via snippet]`; [Nextgov/FCW](https://www.nextgov.com/artificial-intelligence/2026/06/anthropic-suspends-top-ai-models-after-us-export-control-order/414173/)
  `[via snippet]`) Reporting attributes the order's trigger to **another company's claim that it had
  jailbroken Mythos** — alarming officials about misuse potential; Anthropic counters that the
  jailbreak it believes is being cited was *narrow* (unlocking one specific cybersecurity capability),
  not a universal defeat of the models' safeguards. ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/13/us-orders-anthropic-to-disable-ai-models-for-all-foreign-nationals)
  `[via snippet]`) Why it matters: this is the first time export-control machinery built for *chips*
  has been pointed at access to a *deployed model* — a precedent with large implications for cloud
  AI, for any multinational using Claude, and for the open-vs-closed balance. It is also the direct
  backdrop to today's GLM-5.2 open-weights drop, which multiple outlets framed explicitly as the
  Chinese open-weight ecosystem's "you can't ban a download" answer. Well-triangulated across five
  independent outlets; the *interpretation* of scope and the jailbreak specifics remain contested
  between Anthropic and the government. `[disputed]`
