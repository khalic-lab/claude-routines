  Google model, reportedly ~$1B/year, in Tim Cook's final keynote.** Multiple outlets converge on

  renting it — a striking admission that catching up to GPT-/Gemini-class assistants in-house was
  not achievable on Apple's timeline. The keynote is also **Tim Cook's last as CEO** (he steps down
  1 September; John Ternus succeeds him), which raises the stakes for a clean reveal. —

## 💼 Industry, funding & regulation

The live non-Apple thread today is the **trillion-dollar IPO test** — two of the most valuable
private AI-adjacent companies heading for public markets within months of each other, a genuine
referendum on whether public investors will underwrite AI valuations at this scale.

- **SpaceX/xAI (ticker SPCX) prices its IPO Wednesday at a fixed $135/share — targeting a ~$1.75
  trillion valuation and up to a $75B raise, which would be the largest IPO in history.** The entity
  is the **February merger of SpaceX and Elon Musk's AI lab xAI** (the merger itself was struck at a
  ~$1.25T combined valuation), so xAI — maker of the Grok models — is the AI half of what goes
  public. Reporting has the company **skipping the usual price-range roadshow and going straight to a
  fixed $135**, with ~555.6M Class A shares sold to raise about $75B, pricing **11 June** and
  beginning Nasdaq trading **12 June** under **SPCX**. At $75B raised it would surpass Saudi Aramco's
  2019 record (~$35.4B); Goldman Sachs leads alongside Morgan Stanley, BofA, Citi, and JPMorgan.
  *Why it matters:* it forces public-market price discovery onto an AI-bearing megacap for the first
  time at this size — the number the market actually pays will reset the comp for every other AI
  raise, up or down. — [CNBC, 2026-06-03](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/spacex-ipo-stock-price-roadshow-musk.html) `[via snippet]`

- **OpenAI's own confidential IPO filing puts a second trillion-dollar AI listing on the autumn
  calendar.** OpenAI confidentially filed a draft registration statement (an **S-1** submitted to the
  SEC privately, so financials aren't yet public) in **late May** with Goldman Sachs and Morgan
  Stanley as lead underwriters, targeting a listing window between roughly **September and November
  2026**. Its last private valuation was ~$850B, and bankers on the deal expect the float could push
  it past **$1 trillion**. *Why it matters:* back-to-back SPCX and OpenAI debuts mean the second half
  of 2026 becomes the moment AI valuations stop being a private-round abstraction and get marked to
  an actual public bid — and SPCX on Wednesday is the leading indicator for how OpenAI's autumn
  reception will go. — [CNBC, 2026-05-20](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/openai-ipo-filing.html) `[via snippet]` `[ongoing — filed late May, listing window Sep–Nov]`
