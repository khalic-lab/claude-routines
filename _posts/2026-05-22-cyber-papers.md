- **arXiv:2605.22438** (submitted ~21 May 2026, announced 22 May) — *Do Not Trust The Auctioneer: Learning to Bid in Feedback-Manipulated Auctions* — An RL and online-learning result for repeated first-price auctions in which the auctioneer plants shill bids that inflate the observed losing bid without changing actual allocation. The learner therefore wins or loses on the true competing bid but observes a distorted signal post-loss. The algorithm combines a robust interval-elimination branch (ignoring shilled reports; achieves Õ(T^{2/3}) regret) with an optimistic branch (debiases losing-side reports via intermittent low-shill events; achieves Õ(√T) when reliable). Practically relevant to ad-exchange bidding where DSPs routinely suspect shill-bid manipulation by SSPs. [preprint] [via snippet] → [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22438)

- **arXiv:2605.22549** (submitted 21 May 2026, announced 22 May) — *A Martingale Kernel Independence Test* — Proposes studentised HSIC statistics whose null distribution is asymptotically standard normal under independence, eliminating the permutation-calibration step that makes HSIC calibration O(B × n²) per test in practice (B ≈ 1,000 permutations common). A single normal-quantile lookup replaces the permutation loop, cutting per-test wall-clock cost by roughly two orders of magnitude. The construction uses a lower-triangular martingale sum of the Hadamard product of empirically centred Gram matrices; bounded-fourth-moment kernels suffice for the central-limit result. Directly relevant to fast independence testing in anomaly-detection and causal-discovery pipelines where many tests run per second. [preprint] [via snippet] → [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22549)

- **arXiv:2605.22795** (submitted ~21–22 May 2026, announced 22 May) — *Finite-Particle Convergence Rates for Conservative and Non-Conservative Drifting Models* — Analyses one-step generative models ("drifting models") in which a source distribution is transported to a target. Proposes a conservative drifting method that replaces the standard displacement-based velocity with a KDE-score-gradient velocity, making the flow field a gradient field (hence conservative). Proves continuous-time finite-particle convergence bounds on ℝ^d; the non-conservative regime additionally requires a scale-alignment residual term that quantifies how far the Laplace displacement deviates from an exact score-difference field. Relevant to the theoretical grounding of flow-matching and shortcut-diffusion generative models where finite-sample convergence guarantees are currently weak. [preprint] [via snippet] → [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22795)
