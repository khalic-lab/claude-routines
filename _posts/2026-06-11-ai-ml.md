## 📊 Benchmarks & evaluations

No new benchmark *paper* or leaderboard methodology dropped today; what follows is the current
state of the public leaderboards as they stand on 11 June, for orientation.

- **SWE-Bench Pro:** the leaderboard is currently topped by **Claude Fable 5 at 80.3%**, with
  the next model ~11 points behind and **GPT-5.5 at 58.6%**. SWE-Bench Pro is the harder,
  contamination-resistant sibling of SWE-Bench Verified — an agent is given a real software
  repository and a real bug/feature issue and must produce a patch that passes the project's
  own tests. A ~22-point gap over GPT-5.5 is large for this benchmark (not chart-padding), but
  read it with care: these are largely **vendor-published numbers** from the Fable 5 launch,
  and independent re-runs typically land a few points lower. [Independent leaderboard, Morph, 2026-06](https://www.morphllm.com/swe-bench-pro) `[via snippet]`

- **LMArena (text):** **Grok-4.1 Thinking** (xAI) currently leads the human-preference Elo
  leaderboard at **~1483**. LMArena ranks models by blind head-to-head human votes (Elo, the
  chess rating system), so it measures *which answer people prefer*, not correctness — a model
  can top it by being more agreeable or better-formatted rather than more accurate.
  [LMArena via llm-stats, 2026-06](https://llm-stats.com/benchmarks/lmarena-text) `[via snippet]` `[single-source]`

- **LiveBench:** **o3-mini** (OpenAI) leads the published LiveBench snapshot at **0.846**.
  LiveBench is deliberately contamination-limited — it swaps in fresh questions every month
  drawn from recent arXiv papers, news and datasets, so models can't have memorised the
  answers from training. The standings here lag the very newest models (the public snapshot
  shows a small evaluated set), so treat it as a floor on "what's been independently re-tested,"
  not the live frontier. [LiveBench via llm-stats, 2026-06](https://llm-stats.com/benchmarks/livebench) `[via snippet]` `[single-source]`

_The standing caveat:_ agentic and coding benchmarks remain easy to over-fit and easy to
report selectively. "Beats model X on benchmark Y" is only meaningful once an independent
party re-runs Y under the same harness — which is precisely why the MiniMax M3 numbers above
sit in limbo until the weights ship.

## 💼 Industry, funding, regulation

This is where 11 June actually happened.

- **Bezos's Prometheus raises $12B at a $41B valuation.** Jeff Bezos's industrial-AI startup
  **Project Prometheus** raised **$12 billion** at a **$41 billion** post-money valuation, from
  a roster including **JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, BlackRock** and Bezos himself. Bezos is co-CEO
  alongside **Vik Bajaj** (a Stanford School of Medicine professor and Verily co-founder). The
  company — ~150 staff across San Francisco, London and **Zurich** — is explicitly *not* a
  robotics play: it is building what Bezos called an "artificial general engineer," i.e.
  AI tooling to help engineers design and manufacture physical products, framed as a "very
  modern version of CAD" (computer-aided design). This follows a **$6.2B** launch round in
  November. Well triangulated across Bloomberg, Axios, CNBC and GeekWire.
  [Axios, 2026-06-11](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/11/prometheus-bezos-industrial-ai) ·
  [GeekWire, 2026-06-11](https://www.geekwire.com/2026/bezos-ai-startup-prometheus-raises-12b-at-41b-valuation-and-the-ceos-explain-what-theyre-doing/) ·

- **SpaceX/xAI prices the largest IPO in history tonight** `[ongoing since 2026-06-07]`. The
  Musk entity formed by SpaceX's all-stock acquisition of **xAI** (Grok + X) in February
  **prices its IPO after the US close today, 11 June**, with Nasdaq trading beginning **12 June**

  above Tesla — and the raise eclipses Saudi Aramco's 2019 record ($29.4B). The AI relevance:
  xAI (the Grok platform and the 220k+ GPU **Colossus** datacentre) is now a segment of a

- **AMD, Dell & Cambridge launch a UK "Sovereign AI Innovation Lab" (SAIL).** AMD, Dell and the
  **University of Cambridge** announced **SAIL**, a UK lab for open, interoperable AI
  infrastructure built on AMD platforms (AMD **ROCm** software stack). It is anchored by the
  newly-launched **£36M Zenith** supercomputer — funded by the UK government, built by Dell,
  powered by **5th-Gen AMD EPYC** CPUs and **AMD Instinct MI355X** GPU accelerators (AMD's
  current datacentre AI GPU), and hosted at Cambridge's Ray Dolby Centre. Zenith gives Cambridge
  a ~6× boost in supercomputing capacity and sits alongside the **Sunrise** fusion-AI system
  built with the UK Atomic Energy Authority. Target workloads: healthcare, climate modelling,
  materials science, engineering simulation and fusion research. "Sovereign AI" here means
  compute the UK controls domestically rather than renting from US hyperscalers — a recurring
  2026 policy theme. [GOV.UK, 2026-06-11](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/cambridge-supercomputer-set-to-get-6-times-more-powerful-as-government-backs-british-ai-innovation) ·
  [Data Center Dynamics, 2026-06-11](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/cambridge-university-launches-36m-zenith-supercomputer/) `[via snippet]`
  - _Why it matters:_ it is a rare frontier-scale **non-NVIDIA** training/inference win — AMD
    Instinct in a flagship national academic machine — and a concrete data point in Europe's
    push for sovereign compute ahead of the EU AI Act becoming fully applicable in August.
