## 🇨🇭 Switzerland & Vaud

- **The rear Lötschental is reachable again, a year after the Blatten rockslide buried the valley floor.** From this weekend (Saturday 4 July 2026) a Postauto (postal bus) runs several times a day from the Lauchernalp up to the Fafleralp — the tourist heart of the valley that had been all but cut off since the May 2025 landslide destroyed the village of Blatten and several hotels. The route uses a newly asphalted emergency road that passes below the Öigschtchummu glacier, which is monitored for collapses; a **traffic-light warning system** can halt buses if the ice moves. Capacity is capped (eight runs, 20 seats a day, booking required) and the CHF 500,000 service is jointly funded by Lötschental Tourismus and the canton of Valais. The valley's largest hotel, on the Fafleralp, reopened Saturday 4 July after a year closed. ([SRF, 4 Jul 2026](https://www.srf.ch/news/schweiz/nach-bergsturz-von-blatten-aufatmen-im-hinteren-loetschental-der-oev-ist-zurueck))
- **Switzerland added roughly 300,000 jobs in five years — but the post-pandemic hiring boom is cooling.** A Federal Statistical Office (BFS) review of Q4 2020–Q4 2025 finds employment at a record high: 5.4 million people in work by the population survey (+5%), or 5.5 million by the firm-based count (+7.5%). Almost all the net growth came from abroad — the foreign labour force rose 15% to about 2 million, while the number of Swiss nationals in work barely moved (+0.4% to 3.6 million); without ~120,000 naturalisations over the period the foreign share would have climbed even more (+21.8%). Momentum is fading, though: open vacancies fell from 123,000 in 2022 to 86,000 at the end of 2025 and are still trending down. A concrete data point for the free-movement debate — the growth is real, and it is overwhelmingly immigration-driven. ([SRF/BFS, 4 Jul 2026](https://www.srf.ch/news/wirtschaft/arbeitsmarkt-schweiz-300-000-neue-stellen-jobwunder-nach-corona-verblasst-langsam))
