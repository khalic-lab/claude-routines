## 🏢 Lab blogs & official releases

- **OpenAI launches "Patch the Planet," an AI-assisted push to fix bugs in core open-source
  software.** [vendor PR] The initiative — part of OpenAI's "Daybreak" security programme, run
  jointly with security firm Trail of Bits — pairs OpenAI's cyber-tuned models (Codex Security and
  a "GPT‑5.5‑Cyber" variant) with full-time human security engineers to find *and* patch
  vulnerabilities in widely depended-on projects. The framing matters: OpenAI explicitly notes that
  AI is now flooding maintainers with more vulnerability reports than they can triage, and pitches
  this as reducing that burden rather than adding to it — engineers vet findings before they reach
  maintainers and help write the fixes and tests. Initial participants are heavyweight infrastructure
  projects: cURL, the Go language project, Python and python.org, pyca/cryptography, Sigstore (software
  supply-chain signing), aiohttp, NATS Server, and freenginx. OpenAI says Trail of Bits engineers
  working with these tools across 19 projects have already flagged "hundreds" of issues and merged
  "dozens" of patches, with more under coordinated disclosure; participating projects get ChatGPT Pro,
  conditional Codex Security access, and API credits. *Why it matters:* this is the first at-scale test
  of the "AI finds bugs faster than humans can fix them" problem that's been looming all year — the
  interesting part isn't the discovery (LLMs have been doing that), it's whether a lab + security-firm
  pipeline can actually close the loop to *merged patches* on software that sits under most of the
  internet. Treat the "hundreds/dozens" counts as vendor-reported until the patches and disclosures are
  public. ([OpenAI, 22 Jun 2026](https://openai.com/index/patch-the-planet/))

## 🚀 New models, datasets, & open weights

The weekend's notable open-weight releases skew small and task-specific — document understanding and
on-device reasoning rather than new flagship LLMs.

- **Baidu releases Unlimited-OCR, a compact multilingual document model.** A ~3.3‑billion-parameter
  vision-language model (it reads images/PDFs and emits text) published under the permissive MIT
  license. Baidu has been one of the more active Chinese labs in open document-AI, and a 3B OCR
  (optical character recognition — turning page images into machine-readable text) model is small
  enough to run locally. *Why it matters:* multilingual OCR that you can self-host matters for anyone
  digitising documents without shipping them to a cloud API; the MIT license means no usage strings
  attached. Capability claims aren't yet independently benchmarked — this is the model card, not an
  evaluation. ([Hugging Face model card, updated 22 Jun 2026](https://hf.co/baidu/Unlimited-OCR))

- **Datalab ships "lift," a ~9.7B PDF-to-structured-data extractor.** Built on the Qwen3.5
  architecture, `lift` is purpose-trained to pull structured JSON (names, tables, fields) out of PDF
  page images rather than to chat. Released under OpenRAIL (a "responsible AI" license with
  use-restriction clauses — note it's *not* a no-strings open license like MIT/Apache). *Why it
  matters:* "turn this messy PDF into clean JSON" is one of the highest-value boring tasks in
  enterprise AI, and a dedicated ~10B model that does it well can beat a much larger general model on
  cost and reliability. ([Hugging Face model card, 19 Jun 2026](https://hf.co/datalab-to/lift))

- **Weibo's AI lab releases VibeThinker-3B, a tiny reasoning model.** [preprint] A 3‑billion-parameter
  model finetuned from Qwen2.5-Coder-3B, MIT-licensed, aimed at math, code, and reasoning (its card
  cites GPQA — a graduate-level science Q&A benchmark — and instruction-following), with an
  accompanying preprint (arXiv:2606.16140). The bet behind sub‑4B "thinker" models is that careful
  reasoning-focused finetuning can make a model small enough for a laptop punch above its parameter
  count on structured problems. *Why it matters:* if a 3B model can hold its own on math/reasoning, it
  changes what's runnable on-device. The benchmark claims are the lab's own and the paper is a
  preprint (not peer-reviewed) — worth watching, not yet a settled result. ([Hugging Face model card,
  19 Jun 2026](https://hf.co/WeiboAI/VibeThinker-3B))

MLX (Apple's array framework) and GGUF (the llama.cpp quantised-weights format) ports of last week's
`gemma-4-12B` and GLM-5.2 releases continue to dominate Hugging Face's trending list, which is the
normal "community quantises last week's drop" pattern rather than new news.

## 💼 Industry, funding, regulation

- **AI's intramural rivalry has spilled into the US midterms — and the first real test is tomorrow (Tuesday 23 June).**
  AI-focused super PACs (political action committees that can raise and spend unlimited money
  independently of campaigns) have already spent **$43.3 million** on congressional races this cycle,
  per spending-tracker OpenSecrets. The flashpoint is a New York City congressional primary on
  **Tuesday 23 June** for Alex Bores, a former Palantir engineer pushing New York's RAISE Act to more
  tightly regulate AI: groups tied to OpenAI and Anthropic have collectively spent **over $15 million**
  for and against him, per Federal Election Commission filings. The two camps mirror the corporate
  fight — **Leading the Future** (funded mainly by Andreessen Horowitz, an OpenAI investor, and
  OpenAI president Greg Brockman) has raised >$75M and spent $23.5M opposing stricter rules across
  states from Texas to Montana; **Public First**, which Anthropic seeded with $20M in February and
  which favours leaving room for state-level safeguards, has spent $16.6M the other way. Meta, Google,
  and crypto investor Chris Larsen are funding their own AI-policy PACs. *Why it matters:* this is the
  AI industry's first big experiment in whether campaign cash can shape *who writes the eventual rules*
  — and it's happening while federal AI legislation remains stalled in Congress. For lobbying scale:
  OpenAI, Meta, Alphabet, and Nvidia spent a combined $50.9M lobbying Congress in 2025; Anthropic more
  than quadrupled its Q1 lobbying to $1.56M, OpenAI nearly doubled to $1.02M. Both OpenAI and Anthropic
  are reportedly planning large IPOs later this year. (NPR is a secondary report; the underlying figures
  are FEC filings and OpenSecrets/Issue One tallies.) ([NPR, 22 Jun 2026](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/22/nx-s1-5856359/ai-anthropic-congress-spending-openai-midterms-election))
