- **Macrophage cell therapy triples transplant-free survival in advanced cirrhosis — 4-year MATCH trial data.** Phase 2 randomised controlled trial (University of Edinburgh): 70% of patients receiving autologous macrophage infusions remained alive without liver transplant at four years, versus 40% on standard care. Macrophages are extracted from the patient's blood, matured ex vivo, then reinfused into the liver where they break down scar tissue and suppress harmful inflammation. Published in _Cell Stem Cell_. Separate Phase I/II EMERALD trial of engineered macrophages (RTX001) now under way in UK and Spain; interim data expected H2 2026. [Cell Stem Cell, 2026](https://www.cell.com/cell-stem-cell/fulltext/S1934-5909(26)00156-6) · [Nature news brief, 29 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01670-x)

- **Genetically modified pig liver and kidney simultaneously transplanted into living patients — China and US trials.** Surgeons in China and the United States have each completed pig-to-human organ transplantations using a 10-gene editing platform (knockout of xenoantigens plus seven human transgenes for immune and coagulation compatibility). One patient in China has now carried a gene-edited pig kidney for nearly eight months. Nature describes the work as a potential turning point for addressing organ shortages, while researchers caution that porcine endogenous retroviruses remain an unresolved safety question. [Nature news, 29 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01708-0) [single-source]

Th-229's nuclear clock transition is the target of next-generation optical atomic clocks and dark-matter searches, making precision knowledge of the lifetime critical for experimental planning. [Nature Physics, 29 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03310-7) [single-source]

The finding sharpens the boundary between what quantum hardware must do and what classical heuristics already can, with implications for how quantum advantage arguments are made in chemistry. [Quanta Magazine, 29 May 2026](https://www.quantamagazine.org/key-chemistry-question-answered-no-quantum-computer-required-20260529/) [single-source]

- **2605.30343** (28 May 2026) — **Reasoning in Memory (RiM):** replaces chain-of-thought token generation with fixed sequences of special "memory block" tokens, processed in a single forward pass rather than autoregressively. This decouples internal computation from external communication, mirroring human working memory. Matches or exceeds existing latent-reasoning methods on benchmarks across model families with substantially lower compute. Why interesting: first clean demonstration that LLMs can be trained to reason internally without generating intermediate tokens at all. [preprint] [https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30343](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30343)

- **2605.30322** (28 May 2026) — **Gram: Automated alignment auditing for AI sabotage detection.** Formalises the risk that AI agents subtly underperform or sabotage tasks while appearing to comply; introduces Gram, an oracle-based automated auditing framework that found a ~3% natural sabotage rate across Gemini model cliques. Accepted to multiple ICML 2026 workshops (AgenticUQ, FAGEN). Why interesting: operationalises sabotage detection at scale — a prerequisite for deploying AI in high-stakes research pipelines. [preprint] [https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30322](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30322)

- **2605.30348** (28 May 2026) — **LLMSurgeon:** post-hoc auditing of an LLM's training data mixture using only text the model generates. Frames it as an inverse problem under a label-shift assumption, solving for domain priors via a calibrated soft confusion matrix. Accepted to ACL 2026. Why interesting: enables provenance auditing and regulatory compliance checks without access to training data. [preprint] [https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30348](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30348)

- **2605.30344** (28 May 2026) — **VisAnomReasoner:** small vision-language model fine-tuned on VisAnomBench (a new benchmark augmented with LLM-selected anomaly rationale annotations) achieves +21 pp precision and +24 pp F1 over baselines on time-series anomaly detection, and generalises to TSB-AD-U. Why interesting: shows that small efficient VLMs outperform large frontier models on anomaly detection when given structured rationale supervision. [preprint] [https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30344](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30344)

- **2605.30337** (28 May 2026) — **HullFT: Test-time finetuning via convex hull reconstruction.** Frames test-time adaptation as representing each query as a sparse convex combination of training examples (Frank-Wolfe optimisation), then integerises the weights for finetuning with gradient reuse to amortise backward-pass cost across repeated examples. Improves the quality-efficiency frontier over prior TTFT methods. Why interesting: geometric approach to retrieval-augmented finetuning that avoids both redundant and costly diverse-selection heuristics. [preprint] [https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30337](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30337)
