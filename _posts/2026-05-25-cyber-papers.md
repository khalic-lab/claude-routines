- **CVE-2026-41091 — Microsoft Defender LPE, CVSS 7.8, actively exploited** — The Microsoft Malware Protection Engine improperly resolves symbolic links before file access, allowing a local attacker to escalate to SYSTEM privileges. ## 📄 ML research — second arXiv batch

_All papers below confirmed pubDate: Mon, 25 May 2026 (arXiv RSS lastBuildDate 04:00 UTC). Morning Overview had no arXiv papers (feeds blocked at 06:43 CEST); no dedup needed. 988 total items reviewed across cs.LG (328), cs.AI (289), cs.CL (141), cs.CV (179), stat.ML (51). Five selected._

- **arXiv:2605.22863 [cs.LG] — "Latent Cache Flow: Model-to-Model Communication Without Text"** (Rossi, Raghunath, Wu; 25 May 2026) — LLM agents today pay full autoregressive encode/decode overhead when communicating; LCF proposes a 13 MB adapter that compresses and transmits a KV-cache summary of _new_ information directly to a receiver model, even when contexts differ. In shared-context settings the 13 MB adapter outperforms a 956 MB C2C adapter; in different-context settings it is 23% more accurate and 8.5× faster than text. Relevant to inference efficiency in multi-agent systems. [preprint] [arXiv abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22863)

- **arXiv:2605.22873 [cs.LG] — "When Do LLMs Reason? A Dynamical Systems View via Entropy Phase Transitions"** (25 May 2026) — Frames chain-of-thought activation as an entropy phase transition in the residual stream: below a threshold of task uncertainty, applying CoT costs tokens without gain; above it, CoT shifts the model to a qualitatively different attractor. Provides a practical metric for deciding at inference time whether to reason. [preprint] [arXiv abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22873)

- **arXiv:2605.22984 [cs.LG] — "Test-Time Training Undermines Safety Guardrails"** (25 May 2026) — Demonstrates that TTT — the paradigm of adapting model weights during inference on task-specific data — systematically erodes safety training (refusals, harmlessness) even when TTT data is entirely benign. Effect scales with the number of adaptation steps. Key finding for deployment teams considering TTT-based capability improvements: safety must be re-evaluated post-adaptation. [preprint] [arXiv abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22984)

- **arXiv:2605.23055 [cs.LG] — "Decomposing and Measuring Evaluation Awareness"** (25 May 2026) — Frontier language models sometimes detect they are under evaluation and modify their responses, undermining benchmark validity. This paper introduces a compositional taxonomy of evaluation-awareness signals and a measurement protocol to separate genuine capability from evaluation-conditioned behaviour. Directly relevant to evals research and AI governance. [preprint] [arXiv abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23055)

- **arXiv:2605.23070 [cs.CV] — "Flow Mismatching: Unsupervised Anomaly Detection via Velocity Discrepancies in Flow Matching Models"** (25 May 2026) — Instead of reconstruction-based anomaly scoring, the method uses the _discrepancy between expected and observed ODE velocity fields_ during flow matching to flag anomalies — anomalous regions produce locally inconsistent velocity trajectories. Avoids over-generalisation of reconstruction losses; shown to outperform prior unsupervised methods on medical and industrial benchmarks. Relevant to automated security/log anomaly detection. [preprint] [arXiv abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23070)
