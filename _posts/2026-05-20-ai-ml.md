**Google I/O 2026: Gemini 3.5 Flash, Antigravity 2.0, and the "agent runtime" pivot**

Google's annual developer conference (May 19–20) was dominated by agentic product launches. The clearest framing from the keynote: Gemini is no longer a chatbot — it is an agent execution platform.

- **Gemini 3.5 Flash** reached general availability at I/O. Priced at **$1.50/M input tokens and $9.00/M output tokens** (cached input: $0.15/M) — roughly 3× the cost of Gemini 3 Flash ($0.50/$3.00). CEO Sundar Pichai cited 289 tokens/sec on-stage. Google's own benchmark slide: 76.2% on Terminal-Bench 2.1 (vs. 70.3% for 3.1 Pro), 83.6% on MCP Atlas, 1,656 Elo on GDPval-AA. Available across the Gemini API, AI Studio, Antigravity, Vertex AI/Gemini Enterprise, the Gemini app, and AI Mode in Search. A **Gemini 3.5 Pro** is in internal testing and slated for release next month. [vendor PR] — [MarkTechPost, 20 May 2026](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/05/20/google-introduces-gemini-3-5-flash-at-i-o-2026-a-faster-and-cheaper-model-for-ai-agents-and-coding/) [via snippet]; [Simon Willison, 19 May 2026](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/19/gemini-35-flash/) [via snippet]

- **Antigravity 2.0** launched as a standalone multi-agent development suite — desktop app, CLI, SDK, Managed Agents in the Gemini API, and an enterprise tier (Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform). Core feature: hierarchical subagent orchestration where a primary "project manager" agent delegates to specialised sub-agents (Architect, Coding, Testing, Documentation). Developers can spin up an isolated Linux execution environment with a single Gemini API call. Scheduling support added for off-hours autonomous work (dependency updates, refactoring). A new **AI Ultra plan at $100/month** (5× Antigravity quota vs. AI Pro) accompanies the launch; $100 bonus credits for new subscribers through May 25. [vendor PR] — [TechCrunch, 19 May 2026](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-launches-antigravity-2-0-with-an-updated-desktop-app-and-cli-tool-at-io-2026/) [via snippet]; [9to5Google, 19 May 2026](https://9to5google.com/2026/05/19/google-antigravity-agentic-developer-suite/) [via snippet]

- **Gemini Spark** — a persistent AI agent inside the Gemini app that runs continuously on dedicated Google Cloud VMs, completing long-running tasks and integrating with connected apps without requiring the user's device to remain open. The Gemini app crossed **900 million monthly active users**, more than doubling from 400M a year ago. [vendor PR] — [CNBC, 19 May 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/19/google-ai-ultra-gemini-spark-omni.html) [via snippet]

## 💼 Industry, funding & regulation

- **EU AI Act Omnibus — high-risk deadlines delayed 16 months** (May 7). In the first formal amendments to the EU AI Act since its June 2024 adoption, Council and Parliament reached a provisional agreement: Annex III high-risk AI (use-based) obligations pushed from **August 2, 2026 → December 2, 2027**; Annex I high-risk AI (product-regulated: radio equipment, lifts, medical devices) pushed from **August 2027 → August 2028**. Rationale: CEN-CENELEC standards bodies need additional time to develop the technical standards underpinning compliance requirements. Two new prohibitions added, effective December 2, 2026: AI systems generating non-consensual intimate imagery and CSAM. Transparency/watermarking compliance window tightened from 6 months to 3, with a December 2, 2026 deadline. — [EU Council press release, 7 May 2026](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/) [via snippet]; [Hogan Lovells analysis](https://www.hoganlovells.com/en/publications/eu-legislators-agree-to-delay-for-highrisk-ai-rules) [via snippet]

## 🍎 Apple Silicon / on-device

- **MLX vs. llama.cpp: the 40K-token ceiling quantified.** A May 2026 Towards AI analysis measured Ollama 0.19's MLX backend (switched from llama.cpp on March 30) at up to **3× faster token generation** for standard workloads on Apple Silicon — but found the advantage largely disappears beyond ~40K tokens, where MLX's memory management patterns become less favourable. The March 30 switch had delivered headline gains: 58 → 112 tokens/sec on M4 Pro for standard models. Practical implication: for sub-40K context use cases (most chat, coding, RAG), MLX is clearly superior on Apple Silicon; for long-context production workloads (e.g. Kimi K2.6's 262K window), the picture is more nuanced. [single-source for the 40K threshold figure] — [Towards AI / Chew Loong Nian, May 2026](https://pub.towardsai.net/apples-mlx-runs-local-llms-3x-faster-than-llama-cpp-until-your-context-hits-40k-715ec441afbb) [via snippet]; [Ollama blog, 30 Mar 2026](https://ollama.com/blog/mlx) [via snippet]
