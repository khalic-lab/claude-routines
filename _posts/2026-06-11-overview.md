## 🔬 Science

- **JWST resolves the morning-vs-evening atmosphere of an ultra-hot Jupiter.** Using the James Webb Space Telescope, a team led by Cyril Gapp (Max Planck Institute for Astronomy, Heidelberg) measured distinct chemistry on the two terminators — the dawn and dusk rings that divide day from night — of WASP-121 b, a "ultra-hot Jupiter" (a gas giant orbiting so close its dayside tops ~2,500 °C). The evening side absorbs more starlight than the morning side, and the carbon-monoxide signal rises while water drops, both consistent with fierce eastward winds dragging heat around the planet. Why it matters: this is among the first times a single exoplanet's weather has been read separately on its two edges rather than as one blurred average, a real step toward 3-D exoplanet climate maps. Published in _Nature Astronomy_. ([phys.org, 2026-06-10](https://phys.org/news/2026-06-jwst-reveals-dawn-dusk-atmosphere.html)) `[via snippet]`

- **A tabletop interferometer detects gravity's pull on single photons.** A group including Haocun Yu (Univ. of Tennessee / Univ. of Vienna) built a 50-km-path Mach-Zehnder fiber interferometer — light split down two arms and recombined so tiny path differences show up as interference — running at the single-photon level, and measured the optical phase shift that gravity imprints on the light (a lab-scale gravitational redshift, the slowing of light's phase in a gravitational potential). Why it matters: probing how gravity acts on individual quantum particles is exactly the regime where quantum mechanics and general relativity are hardest to reconcile, and doing it on a bench rather than at astronomical scale opens a cheap experimental handle on that frontier. Honest scope: this measures a known general-relativistic effect with new quantum-level precision — it is not yet a test that distinguishes competing quantum-gravity theories. Published in _Physical Review Letters_. ([phys.org, 2026-06](https://phys.org/news/2026-06-tabletop-fundamental-physics.html)) `[via snippet]` `[single-source]`

- **An ancient cyanobacteria lineage exposes an early rung on the ladder to photosynthesis.** Quanta reports on basal (deep-branching, early-diverging) cyanobacteria that appear to preserve an intermediate stage of oxygenic photosynthesis — the process that splits water using sunlight and flooded the early Earth with oxygen ~2.4 billion years ago. Why it matters: how cells first learned to harvest light into chemistry is one of biology's deepest origin questions, and living lineages that froze a halfway state are rare windows into a transition normally readable only from fossils and inference. ([Quanta Magazine, 2026-06-10](https://www.quantamagazine.org/an-early-step-on-the-long-strange-road-to-photosynthesis-20260610/))

- **Dual-target gene therapy for Parkinson's clears its phase 1 safety bar.** A multicenter, open-label phase 1 trial of BBM-P002 — an adeno-associated-virus vector (AAV; a harmless engineered virus used to ferry genes into cells) co-delivering two dopamine-making enzymes, tyrosine hydroxylase and AADC — reported 12-month safety and tolerability in 10 people with moderate-to-advanced Parkinson's after bilateral infusion into the putamen (a deep brain motor-control region). Why it matters: most Parkinson's gene therapies install one enzyme; co-delivering two aims to rebuild more of the dopamine-synthesis pathway in one shot, and early motor improvements are an encouraging (if small, uncontrolled) signal. Phase 1 = safety first, not proof of efficacy. _Nature Medicine_. ([Nature Medicine, 2026-06-10](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04436-0)) `[via snippet]`

- **A myostatin blocker protects muscle during weight-loss-drug therapy.** In a randomized, double-blind phase 2 trial (102 adults, 24 weeks), apitegromab — an antibody that blocks myostatin, the body's brake on muscle growth — added to tirzepatide (a GLP-1/GIP "weight-loss shot") cut lean-mass loss by 54.9% versus placebo while total weight loss stayed similar. Lean mass made up just 14.6% of the weight lost with apitegromab vs 30.2% with placebo. Why it matters: GLP-1 drugs strip muscle alongside fat, a real concern for older or frail patients; protecting lean mass could make the weight people lose healthier. _Nature Medicine_. ([Nature Medicine, 2026-06-08](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04440-4); [Science Media Centre reaction, 2026-06](https://www.sciencemediacentre.org/expert-reaction-to-study-investigating-a-drug-apitegromab-to-help-to-preserve-lean-body-mass-during-weight-loss-with-tirzepatide/)) `[via snippet]`

## 📄 ML research — first arXiv batch

- **arXiv:2606.11205 (2026-06-11) — sycophancy steering also erases _correct_ agreement.** "Dual-stance evaluation" tests whether a direction that reduces an LLM's sycophancy (telling you what you want to hear) also suppresses agreement with true statements. On Llama-3-8B it does: sycophantic and factual agreement live in geometrically distinct activation subspaces, yet the steering vector projects onto both, so steering away from flattery also makes the model disagree that "the Earth is round." Why interesting: a clean counterexample to the hope that you can surgically edit one behavior via activation steering — readable directions aren't always _writable_ without collateral damage. `[preprint]` ([abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11205))

- **arXiv:2606.11634 (2026-06-11) — RL rescues cheap attention for math reasoning.** SWARR converts a pretrained full-attention model to sliding-window attention — SWA, which only looks at a fixed local span instead of the whole context, dodging the quadratic cost of self-attention — via supervised fine-tuning, then closes the resulting quality gap with reinforcement learning. Why interesting: long-context reasoning is expensive precisely because attention scales with the square of context length; recovering full-attention math performance from a windowed model with RL alone (no retraining the base) is a practical efficiency lever. `[preprint]` ([abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11634))

- **arXiv:2606.11243 (2026-06-11) — hierarchical flow matching beats RFDiffusion at designing enzymes.** ProHiFlo generates proteins coarse-to-fine (backbone first, then all-atom) using flow matching — a diffusion-like generative method that learns a smooth transport from noise to data — with pretrained predictors steering toward desired function without retraining. On enzyme active-site scaffolding (building a protein around a catalytic site) it reports 58.9% success vs 41.2% for RFDiffusion, in 4× fewer sampling steps. Why interesting: a meaningful jump on a hard, practically useful protein-design task, with a cost reduction rather than a cost increase. `[preprint]` ([abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11243))

- **arXiv:2606.11211 (2026-06-11) — thinking longer can make a model _more_ confidently wrong.** "Calibration Drift Under Reasoning" shows that pushing chain-of-thought (CoT; the model reasoning step-by-step before answering) past a task-specific length makes Llama-3.1-8B systematically overconfident — calibration error first falls as reasoning fixes mistakes, then rises as long reasoning spins internally consistent but wrong explanations. They propose CABStop, a rule that halts reasoning when stated confidence diverges from an accuracy estimate. Why interesting: directly challenges "more reasoning = more reliable," with a concrete monitoring fix. Caveat: 8B shows the effect; 70B results were inconclusive. `[preprint]` ([abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11211))

- **arXiv:2606.11213 (2026-06-11) — structured eviction gives agents near-unbounded memory.** Context Window Lifecycle (CWL) keeps a long-running agent inside its token budget without summarizing: the agent tags its history as typed, dependency-linked episodes, and a deterministic (LLM-free) policy drops whichever past actions are already "saved" in the environment, keeping live context near a stable ceiling. Reported: one session running 89 sequential tasks across 80 million tokens with no measurable accuracy loss vs isolated per-task runs. Why interesting: an alternative to summary-based compaction that sidesteps its hallucination and cost problems for long-horizon agents. `[preprint]` ([abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11213))
