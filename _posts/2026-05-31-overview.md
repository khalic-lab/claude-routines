- **A landmark quantum-chemistry problem cracked without a quantum computer.** A result decades in the making shows that ordinary *classical* computers can accurately model certain complex chemical reactions many had assumed would need quantum hardware. Why it matters: it sharpens where a genuine *quantum advantage* (a task a quantum computer provably does faster than any classical machine) actually exists in chemistry — trimming the hype and aiming quantum-computing effort at the problems that truly need it. - **Gut bacteria may flag Parkinson's risk years before symptoms.** Researchers found a slice of the *gut microbiome* (the community of microbes in the intestine) that shifts in a consistent, graded way — from healthy people, through carriers of genetic risk, to patients with overt *Parkinson's disease* (a progressive movement disorder driven by loss of dopamine-producing neurons). Why it matters: a microbiome screen could in principle catch the disease in its silent phase, when future protective treatments would have the best chance. ([Nature Medicine, 2026-05-28](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04458-8))
- **The most comprehensive map yet of Indigenous American genomes.** A large genomic analysis charts the evolutionary history of Indigenous American populations — shaped by geography, climate shifts and colonization — and finds signatures of *natural selection* (genetic variants that spread because they helped survival in local conditions). Why it matters: genomic datasets have long under-represented these populations, which both limits the science and risks leaving them out of genomically guided medicine. ([Nature Medicine, 2026-05-29](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41591-026-00028-0))
- **Sleuths flag 100+ suspect images in a major antibody catalogue.** Image-integrity investigators identified more than 100 questionable images in a Thermo Fisher antibody catalogue, reviving long-standing concern about whether commercial *antibodies* (lab reagents used to detect specific proteins) actually bind what their labels claim. Why it matters: unreliable antibodies are a quiet but pervasive source of irreproducible biomedical results. ([Nature, 2026-05-29](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01706-2))
