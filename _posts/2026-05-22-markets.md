## 🌍 World politics — US morning

- **Kevin Warsh sworn in as Federal Reserve Chair at White House.** [central bank] Trump hosted Warsh's swearing-in in the East Room — the first such ceremony at the White House since Alan Greenspan in 1987. Supreme Court Justice Clarence Thomas administered the oath. Warsh, confirmed 54–45 on May 13 (the most divided Fed confirmation in history), said he will "lead a reform-oriented Federal Reserve, escaping static frameworks and models." Trump told Warsh to be "totally independent," while separately calling publicly for lower rates. Market reaction has been the opposite: bond traders are now fully pricing at least one 25 bp hike by year-end 2026, reflecting fears that inflation (oil above $100, shelter costs accelerating) will force Warsh to tighten despite presidential pressure.
