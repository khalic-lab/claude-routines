## 🔬 Science

- **First-in-human bispecific TCR engager IMA401 (targets a MAGE-A4/MAGE-A8 tumour peptide) reports early efficacy in solid tumours.** A phase 1 trial of IMA401 — a "TCR-based bispecific T-cell engager," i.e. a single antibody-like molecule that grabs a cancer-specific peptide on one arm and a T cell on the other, dragging the immune cell onto the tumour — reports encouraging safety and a preliminary efficacy signal in recurrent/refractory head-and-neck cancer and melanoma, including patients also on anti-PD-1 (a checkpoint-inhibitor immunotherapy). *Why it matters:* MAGE-A4/A8 are "cancer-testis antigens" — proteins normally silent in adult tissue but switched on in many tumours — so a reagent that reliably engages them could extend cell-engager therapy beyond blood cancers into solid tumours, the field's long-standing wall. [single-source] ([Nature Medicine, 2026-05-31](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04455-x))

- **JWST recovers CO, water and OH in the ultra-hot Jupiter WASP-18b using a "direct-extraction" pipeline.** A re-analysis of JWST/NIRISS data pulls atmospheric molecules straight from pixel-level spectra at native resolution (rather than first binning into a low-resolution spectrum), then applies cross-correlation — template-matching a molecule's many spectral lines at once, which boosts sensitivity to faint species. *Why it matters:* the method squeezes more chemistry out of existing data and detecting OH (a fragment of water broken apart by the planet's ~2,400 K heat) helps map how these scorched atmospheres chemically cycle. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30871, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30871))

- **JWST's "overmassive" early black holes look less extreme once AGN light is modelled properly.** JWST has found many active galactic nuclei (AGN — galaxies with an accreting central black hole) at redshift z>4 whose black holes seem far too heavy for their host galaxy's stellar mass versus the local relation. Re-fitting 39 such galaxies (z≈3.5–7) with models that account for the AGN's own glow finds the black-hole-to-stellar-mass ratio still evolves rapidly with cosmic time but is less off-the-charts than naive estimates suggested. *Why it matters:* whether early black holes truly outgrew their galaxies bears directly on how the first supermassive black holes formed — a real tension, partly an artefact of fitting assumptions. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30414, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30414))

- **A denser planar surface code claims up to 4.5× the logical-qubit packing of the standard layout.** The surface code is today's leading scheme for quantum error correction — spreading one reliable "logical" qubit across a 2D grid of noisy physical qubits so errors can be caught. This design "yokes" a dense packing of twist defects (topological features that store quantum information) on a hex grid with a shorter 4-layer stabiliser cycle. *Why it matters:* fewer physical qubits per logical qubit is the single biggest lever on whether fault-tolerant quantum computers are buildable this decade; a 4.5× packing gain (under a standard 10⁻³ circuit-noise model) directly attacks that overhead. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30455, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30455))

- **Gaia quasar proper motions used to clock the Solar System's acceleration through the cosmos.** Distant quasars should sit still, but our Solar System's acceleration makes their apparent positions drift in a tiny, dipole-shaped pattern (relativistic aberration — like rain seeming to slant as you accelerate forward). Using Gaia's quasar catalogue and simulation-based inference, the authors extract this dipole while carefully subtracting systematics tied to Gaia's scanning pattern. *Why it matters:* it's a direct, geometric measurement of our motion relative to the distant universe — a clean cosmological reference that previously needed decades of radio interferometry. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30367, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30367))

## 📄 ML research — first arXiv batch

- **VeriGate — verifier-gated step-level supervision for GRPO.** GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization — the RL recipe behind recent reasoning models, which scores a batch of answers against each other) stalls when every sampled answer to a prompt gets the same verifier reward: the relative advantage collapses to zero and nothing is learned. VeriGate keeps a verifier in charge step-by-step to hand out partial credit along a reasoning chain. *Why interesting:* sparse, answer-only rewards are the main bottleneck in training reasoners; step-level credit could make RL training more sample-efficient and stable. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30451, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30451))

- **Linear probes detect learned deception across five transformer models.** The authors fine-tune honest and deliberately-dishonest variants of five open models (Pythia-1.4B, Gemma-2-2B/9B, Qwen2.5-7B, Llama-3.1-8B) via LoRA (a cheap fine-tuning method that adds small trainable weight patches), then train "linear probes" — simple classifiers reading the model's internal activations — to tell when a model knows the right answer but states a wrong one. *Why interesting:* it's a controlled testbed for "deceptive alignment," the AI-safety worry that a model could hold an accurate internal picture while outputting falsehoods. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30381, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30381))

- **Averaging the outputs of 3–5 LLMs washes out text watermarks.** Text watermarks nudge a model's word-probability distribution in a detectable statistical pattern. The paper proves — and shows empirically — that because rival providers watermark independently, simply averaging their output distributions cancels the perturbations to second order, recovering the un-watermarked text. *Why interesting:* it's a clean impossibility-style result undercutting distributional watermarking as an attribution defence the moment a user can query several models. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30501, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30501))

- **Item Response Theory audit surfaces mislabeled examples in LLM benchmarks.** Borrowing Item Response Theory (IRT — the psychometrics framework behind standardised-test scoring, which jointly models question difficulty and test-taker skill), the authors flag likely-wrong gold labels at 95% precision in the top 200 items across seven preference and multiple-choice benchmarks, using answers from 114 models. *Why interesting:* benchmark labels are frozen at release and silently copied downstream; a cheap mislabel detector means leaderboards may be ranking models against partly-broken answer keys. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30504, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30504))

- **Closed-form reward targets for Best-of-N preference data.** Best-of-N sampling — draw N answers, pair the best with a rejected one — is a standard way to build the preference pairs used to train reward models. This paper derives, in closed form, exactly what a Bradley–Terry reward model (the standard pairwise-preference model) learns from such data as a function of N, and shows it preserves the true reward ranking. *Why interesting:* it turns a widely-used but ad-hoc data-construction trick into something with explicit design rules for choosing N. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30619, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30619))

- **LLMs overwrite local cultural knowledge with globally dominant narratives.** Using CulturalNB, a new dataset of 717 hand-curated Bangla/English question-answer pairs with sourced evidence, nine state-of-the-art models tend to answer culturally grounded Bengali questions with globally prevalent narratives rather than the locally correct ones — even when local evidence is supplied. *Why interesting:* concrete, evidence-backed evidence of cultural homogenisation as LLMs become default knowledge interfaces for low-resource-language users. `[preprint]` ([arXiv:2605.30481, 2026-06-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30481))
