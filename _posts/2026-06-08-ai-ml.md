## 🏢 Lab blogs & official releases

**Apple rebuilds Siri on a custom Google Gemini model — and opens Apple Intelligence to
third-party AI "Extensions" (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok).** `[vendor PR]` The headline
from today's keynote is a strategic reversal. For two years Apple insisted Apple
Intelligence would run on its own models; today it conceded the frontier tier to Google.
The rebuilt Siri is reportedly powered by a *custom ~1.2-trillion-parameter Google Gemini
model* (1.2T = total parameter count, a rough proxy for raw capacity) that Apple has
licensed for a reported ~$1 billion/year, running on Google Cloud's Nvidia Blackwell B200
GPUs (Google's current top-end AI accelerator) for the heaviest requests. Separately — and
arguably the bigger structural story — Apple announced an **Extensions** framework that
lets users set Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini or Grok as their preferred model across system
features (Siri, Writing Tools, Image Playground) via an App Store marketplace, ending the
single-provider ChatGPT arrangement from iOS 18. *Why it matters:* this is the first time
Apple has let a competitor's model be the default brain of the iPhone, and it hands the
labs direct reach to Apple's installed base without per-app deals. Well-triangulated across
post-keynote coverage. ([AI Weekly, 8 Jun 2026](https://aiweekly.co/node/2611) `[via snippet]`;
[Tech Times, 8 Jun 2026](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/317985/20260608/apple-wwdc-2026-siri-rebuilt-gemini-homeos-previewed-cook-farewell-keynote.htm) `[via snippet]`)

**Federighi confirms the Google partnership on-stage: "a deep collaboration with Google."**
`[vendor PR]` Software chief Craig Federighi told the keynote audience Apple had "embarked
on a deep collaboration with Google" to build its next-generation Foundation Models

to run both on-device and through Apple's Private Cloud Compute (Apple's privacy-hardened

three-tier system — trivial requests stay on the phone, mid-complexity go to Private Cloud

Analysis Intelligence Index score to verify "is it actually good." Treat any "Siri now

## 💼 Industry, funding & regulation

**The Apple Extensions decision is a distribution windfall for Anthropic, OpenAI and
Google.** The business story underneath today's keynote: by letting users pick Claude,
ChatGPT, Gemini or Grok as the system default, Apple gives the labs reach to its 1
billion+-device base without each negotiating a bespoke exclusivity deal. Reporting
suggests Gemini is the *default if the user never changes settings* (a meaningful edge,
given how few users touch defaults), with Anthropic and OpenAI as opt-in alternatives. For
Anthropic in particular — which has no consumer hardware and limited consumer reach — this
is the kind of distribution it otherwise can't buy. ([AI Weekly, 8 Jun 2026](https://aiweekly.co/node/2611) `[via snippet]`)

**Pentagon is testing OpenAI, Google and xAI models to replace Anthropic's Claude on
classified systems.** A pointed counter-current to the Apple distribution win, resurfacing
in today's coverage. The Defense Department began formally evaluating rival models after
Anthropic refused to drop two long-standing red lines — no mass surveillance of Americans,
and no fully autonomous weapons that target people without a human in the loop. Claude had
been the primary model on the Pentagon's classified networks (integrated into the Maven
Smart System, the DoD's battlefield-data fusion platform). OpenAI reportedly renegotiated
its DoD terms to permit broader "lawful use," sidestepping the boundaries that cost
Anthropic the contract; Anthropic is challenging the "supply-chain risk" designation in
court. *Why it matters:* it's a clean illustration of the commercial cost of safety
red-lines when the customer is a military buyer. The substance traces to late-May reporting
(Bloomberg, 21 May), so treat today's pieces as re-reporting, not a fresh development.
([Bloomberg, 21 May 2026](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/pentagon-tests-rival-ai-models-in-race-to-replace-anthropic) `[via snippet]`;
[Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/why-replacing-anthropic-with-openai-at-the-pentagon-could-take-months/) `[via snippet]`)

## 🍎 Apple Silicon / on-device

Today's WWDC made this section unusually load-bearing — the on-device story is the part of
the Apple news most relevant to local-inference builders.

**Apple replaces Core ML with a new "Core AI" framework and expands the on-device
Foundation Models API to accept third-party model weights.** Core ML was Apple's decade-old
on-device inference framework (it runs a trained model locally across the Neural Engine,
GPU and CPU). Core AI is its modernized successor with a broader mandate: alongside running
Apple's own models, it introduces a standardized API for developers to **plug in
third-party model weights** and run them locally on Apple Silicon. Paired with an expanded
**Foundation Models** developer API — larger base model than the current 3B, bigger context
window, better fine-tuning support — this is the most consequential on-device-AI move Apple
has made for developers since the framework debuted at WWDC25. *Why it matters:* zero-cost,
no-key, privacy-preserving local inference (data stays on device) becomes available to far
more apps, and the third-party-weights hook means the Mac/iPhone could become a first-class
target for the open-weight models this brief tracks — rather than a walled garden running
only Apple's model. ([AppleInsider — Core AI replaces Core ML](https://appleinsider.com/articles/26/03/01/wwdc-2026-to-introduce-core-ai-as-replacement-for-core-ml) `[via snippet]`;
[Apple Foundation Models docs](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/FoundationModels) `[via snippet]`)

Details to verify once betas land: the new on-device model's size and architecture, whether
"third-party weights" means arbitrary GGUF/MLX models or a curated set, and the real
on-device token/sec (the iOS 26 3B model ran ~30 tok/s on an iPhone 15 Pro). The Weekend
brief is the right place for the depth pass here once developers have hands on the betas.
