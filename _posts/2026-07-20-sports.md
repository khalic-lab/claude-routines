### Spain are world champions — Torres' extra-time strike beats Argentina 1-0 {#st-0cb5583f3808}
**[Spain edge Argentina after extra time to win 2nd World Cup title — ESPN](https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/49403084/2026-world-cup-final-spain-argentina-score-ferran-torres)** · World Cup final · Sun 19 July 2026 · `[via snippet]`
Ferran Torres, on as a substitute, drove home early in extra time (around the 106th minute) to give Spain a 1-0 win over Argentina in the World Cup final at MetLife Stadium outside New York on Sunday. It is Spain's second world title after 2010, and it completes a rare double: they were already European champions, so one nation now holds both trophies at once. The result crowns a young Spanish side built around Lamine Yamal and Nico Williams as the dominant force in the men's game — and it closes the book on Argentina's title defence, and on the 39-year-old Lionel Messi's bid to leave with the trophy in hand. The holders were reduced to ten men (Enzo Fernández sent off) and managed only two shots across 120 minutes; Spain conceded just one goal in the whole tournament, the meanest defence of any World Cup winner, over a record eight matches in the new 48-team format co-hosted by the USA, Canada and Mexico. Torres became only the second substitute ever to score a World Cup final winner.
*Why it matters:* This is the sport's biggest prize, and it settles the argument over who the team of the era is — not Argentina, France or Brazil, but a Spain side young enough to stay there. For a casual viewer, it's the tournament that will frame the next four years of football.
