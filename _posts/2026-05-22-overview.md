## 🔬 Science

- **GLP-1 drugs cut cardiovascular events across 90 000+ patients in landmark meta-analysis** — An Anglia Ruskin University–led pooled analysis of 11 major cardiovascular outcome trials (avg follow-up ~2.8 years, n > 90 000) found GLP-1 receptor agonists significantly reduce heart attacks, strokes, heart failure and all-cause mortality versus placebo. Benefits were most pronounced in patients with obesity but without type 2 diabetes, reinforcing the case for GLP-1s as broad cardiometabolic — not merely glycaemic — agents. Published in _Cardiovascular Diabetology — Endocrinology Reports_. [ScienceDaily, 20 May 2026](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260520093731.htm) [via snippet] [single-source]

- **IDOL enzyme deletion slashes amyloid plaques and restores neuronal resilience in Alzheimer's model** — Ablating the IDOL enzyme from neurons in an animal model sharply reduced amyloid-β burden, elevated APOE-regulatory receptors and improved lipid metabolism and inter-neuronal signalling. Because APOE4 is the strongest known genetic risk factor for late-onset Alzheimer's, the IDOL–APOE receptor axis could offer a multi-pronged therapeutic target — simultaneously attacking plaques and supporting neuronal health. [ScienceDaily, 19–20 May 2026](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260519224334.htm) [via snippet] [single-source]

- **Entanglement area law extended to 1D bosonic systems with long-range interactions and unbounded local energy** — A Nature Communications study proves that area-law entanglement bounds — previously established only for short-range or spin systems — hold for 1D bosonic chains even under long-range interactions and unbounded local energies (Bose-Hubbard class, φ⁴ class). The result also yields efficiency-guaranteed matrix product state (MPS) approximations of ground states for these systems, extending the theoretical scaffold for tensor-network methods. (Preprint: [arXiv:2411.02157](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.02157).) [Nature Communications, 21 May 2026] [via snippet] [single-source]

- **Vision-language models outperform CNNs for neutrino-interaction classification in HEP detectors** — A Nature Communications paper benchmarks a fine-tuned VLM (LLaMA 3.2 family) against state-of-the-art CNN architectures used in NOvA/DUNE-style pixel detectors and finds the VLM achieves higher classification accuracy and greater robustness, while providing interpretable reasoning-based predictions. The work opens a path to deploying general-purpose foundation models directly in particle-physics analyses. (Preprint: [arXiv:2509.08461](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.08461).) [Nature Communications, 21 May 2026] [via snippet] [single-source]

- **arXiv:2605.20936** (submitted 20 May 2026) — *DASH: Fast Differentiable Architecture Search for Hybrid Attention in Minutes on a Single GPU* — Hybrid attention (full-attention layers interleaved with linear/state-space operators) is emerging as the dominant paradigm for inference-efficient LLMs, but existing NAS pipelines require 200B+ tokens (e.g. Jet-Nemotron's PostNAS). DASH relaxes discrete layer-wise operator placement into continuous architecture logits, separating search from final adaptation; each run uses only 12.3M tokens and completes in ~20 min on a single RTX Pro 6000 GPU. [preprint] [via snippet] → [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20936)

- **arXiv:2605.16787** (submitted 16 May 2026) — *The Unlearnability Phenomenon in RLVR for Language Models* — Reveals that during RLVR training a substantial fraction of hard examples is permanently unlearnable even when correct rollouts are available: the unlearnable subset has low gradient similarity with other examples and non-generalizable reasoning patterns. Standard remedies (more rollouts, curriculum) cannot bridge the gap, challenging the assumption that rollout diversity suffices to overcome difficulty. Authors: Yulin Chen, He He, Chen Zhao. [preprint] — [HF Papers confirmed](https://hf.co/papers/2605.16787) → [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16787)

- **arXiv:2605.14366** (submitted ~14 May 2026) — *Reinforcement Learning with Semantic Rewards Enables Low-Resource Language Expansion without Alignment Tax* — Uses semantic-space group relative policy optimisation to extend an instruction-following LLM to a new target language using only a few thousand examples, without degrading the model's existing-language capabilities (the "alignment tax" that plagues standard fine-tuning). Targets practical deployment in low-resource language communities. [preprint] [via snippet] → [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14366)

- **arXiv:2605.07579** (~7–8 May 2026) — *Your Language Model Is Its Own Critic: Reinforcement Learning with Value Estimation from Actor's Internal States* — Proposes using the actor's own hidden-state activations as an implicit value signal, removing the need for a separate critic network in actor-critic RL. Eliminates the critic–actor distribution mismatch and reduces parameter count, with gains on mathematical reasoning benchmarks. [preprint] [via snippet] → [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07579)
