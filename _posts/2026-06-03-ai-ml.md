## 🚀 New models, datasets, & open weights

- **NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Ultra — 550B open weights, dropping June 4.** Announced in Jensen
  Huang's Computex keynote (June 1), Nemotron 3 Ultra is a **550B-parameter** model with
  **55B active** per token, on a **hybrid Mamba-Transformer Mixture-of-Experts** design.
  Quick decoder for the unfamiliar: *Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)* routes each token to a small
  subset of "expert" sub-networks, so the model is huge in total but cheap per token; *Mamba*
  is a state-space-model layer that scales linearly with sequence length (unlike attention's
  quadratic cost), and mixing it with Transformer blocks trades a little quality for a lot of
  speed. NVIDIA says weights **and training recipes** ship June 4 on Hugging Face and its own
  repo. It's billed as the most intelligent **US** open-weights model and runs 3–6× faster
  than Chinese rivals (>300 tokens/s on a pre-release DeepInfra endpoint). *Why it matters:
  an open, agent-oriented frontier model with published training recipes from the GPU vendor
  itself is a direct bid for the open-weights ecosystem — and a US answer to a leaderboard
  China has dominated.* `[vendor PR]` `[via snippet]`
  ([Artificial Analysis, 2026-06-01](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/nvidia-nemotron-3-ultra-launch-announced);
  [The Decoder, 2026-06-02](https://the-decoder.com/nvidias-nemotron-3-ultra-becomes-the-smartest-open-us-model-but-china-still-leads/))

## 💼 Industry, funding, regulation

- **EU drops a "tech-sovereignty" package: Cloud and AI Development Act + Chips Act 2.0.**
  The European Commission proposed a sweep of measures aimed at reducing reliance on US
  cloud, chips, and AI. Centerpiece is the **Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA)**, which aims
  to **at least triple EU data-centre capacity within 5–7 years**, streamline permitting for
  qualifying sites, and define **four cloud/AI "sovereignty assurance levels"** for public
  bodies to pick from by risk: Level 2 requires demonstrated independence from third countries
  and software-supply-chain transparency; Level 3 requires the provider be **EU-owned and
  -controlled**, down to personnel citizenship. Alongside it: a **Chips Act 2.0**, an Open
  Source Strategy, and an energy-digitalisation roadmap. A Commission official framed the goal
  bluntly — "We want to be sure nobody has a kill switch" over European infrastructure. *Why
  it matters: if enacted, the sovereignty levels could effectively wall US hyperscalers out of
  sensitive public-sector contracts — the most concrete "de-risk from US Big Tech" move
  Brussels has made.* `[via snippet]`
  ([European Commission, 2026-06-03](https://commission.europa.eu/news-and-media/news/strengthening-europes-tech-sovereignty-2026-06-03_en);
