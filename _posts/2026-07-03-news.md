- **Ticino freezes ~50M CHF in tax rebates owed to Italy.** On Tuesday 30 June the Ticino cantonal government partially suspended the *rétrocessions fiscales* (the share of frontier-worker withholding tax that Switzerland pays back to Italian border municipalities under the 1974 cross-border accord), blocking some 50 million francs "as a precaution, pending clarifications," said government president Claudio Zali (Lega dei Ticinesi). The move follows weeks of tension with Lombardy and Bern over a health-tax dispute affecting cross-border workers; Bern says it regrets the decision and wants a negotiated solution. Italy protested. ([Le Temps, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.letemps.ch/suisse/tessin/le-tessin-passe-a-l-acte-et-gele-l-argent-a-destination-des-communes-frontalieres-l-italie-proteste))
- **Vaud: Nuria Gorrite will not seek re-election in 2027.** After 15 years in the cantonal executive, the Socialist (PS) minister — head of the culture, infrastructure and HR department, and former Morges mayor — announced she will not run for a fourth term at the February 2027 cantonal election, respecting her party's internal term limits. She said the decision is unrelated to the "affaire Dittli" roiling Vaud politics and left her federal ambitions open. Vaud's three PLR (centre-right) ministers — Luisier, Borloz and Moret — said Thursday they will all stand again. ([Le Temps / ATS, 3 Jul 2026](https://www.letemps.ch/suisse/vaud/nuria-gorrite-quittera-le-conseil-d-etat-vaudois-en-2027))
- **SNB says Swiss banks — including UBS — are well capitalised; UBS calls the report "misleading."** The Swiss National Bank's 2026 Financial Stability Report (released 2 July) concluded the sector is robust and that UBS can meet the Federal Council's proposed 2030 capital rules. Bern's 22 April proposal to make banks fully back foreign subsidiaries with capital would require UBS to add roughly $20bn of CET1 (common equity tier 1 — the highest-quality, first-loss capital), but the SNB notes UBS already exceeds current 2030 requirements by about $13bn and could comply while still paying dividends. UBS pushed back publicly, calling the report misleading and warning the cumulative measures would be a "significant competitive disadvantage."
