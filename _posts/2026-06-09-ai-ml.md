## 🏢 Lab blogs & official releases

**Anthropic releases Claude Fable 5 (public) and Claude Mythos 5 (restricted) — its
most capable generally available model, days after warning AI is getting too
dangerous.** `[vendor PR]` This is the day. Anthropic took its "Mythos"-class model —
previously locked inside **Project Glasswing**, a restricted preview program whose
partners reportedly included AWS, Microsoft, Apple and CrowdStrike — and split it into
two public-facing tiers. **Fable 5** is the generally available version: same underlying
model, but with safeguards that *redirect* certain requests (cybersecurity, biology,
chemistry, and "model distillation" — i.e. using the model to train a competitor) to
the older, tamer **Claude Opus 4.8** instead of answering directly. Anthropic says
those safeguards trigger in *under 5% of sessions* on average. **Mythos 5** is the same
model with some of those guardrails *lifted*, available only to vetted Glasswing
partners in cyberdefense, critical infrastructure and (planned) biomedical research.
The company's stated rationale for the split: the raw model is capable enough at things
like autonomously finding and chaining software exploits that releasing it openly could
do real damage. Anthropic's own line: Fable 5's "capabilities exceed those of any model
we've ever made generally available… state-of-the-art on nearly all tested benchmarks."
Well-triangulated across CNBC, TechCrunch, NBC News, VentureBeat and Inc.

*The pricing and availability mechanics:* Anthropic priced both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 at
**$10 per million input tokens / $50 per million output tokens** — less than half the
price of the earlier Mythos Preview, but still the most expensive major frontier model
on the market (for comparison, that output price is several times GPT-5.5 or Gemini 3.1
Pro). Fable 5 is included on Pro, Max, Team and Enterprise plans **until 22 June 2026**;
after 23 June, usage may require purchased credits "until capacity expands" — a tell
that Anthropic is compute-constrained on this model. It shipped same-day across the
ecosystem: **Amazon Bedrock** (AWS's managed-model service), **GitHub Copilot**, and
legal-AI vendor **Harvey** all announced general availability today. Anthropic did *not*
disclose Fable 5's context-window size or parameter count.
([AWS News Blog, 9 Jun 2026](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/anthropic-claude-fable-5-on-aws-mythos-class-capabilities-with-built-in-safeguards-now-available/) `[via snippet]`;
[GitHub Changelog, 9 Jun 2026](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-06-09-claude-fable-5-is-generally-available-for-github-copilot/) `[via snippet]`)

**The "warn, then ship" contradiction is drawing criticism — and it's a fair hit.**
On 4 June, Anthropic published *"When AI builds itself,"* warning about **recursive
self-improvement** (AI systems accelerating their own development faster than safety and
governance can keep up) and calling for the *option* to slow or temporarily pause
frontier development — but only "if other developers at or near the frontier also did so
in a verifiable manner." No such coordination exists, so the company shipped anyway.
Critics note the convenient positioning: the warning-heavy framing casts Anthropic as
both the *source* of a dangerous new capability and the *gatekeeper* deciding which
governments, companies and researchers get the unrestricted Mythos 5 version. Whether
that's principled risk management or a moat dressed as safety is the debate of the day.
([TechCrunch, 9 Jun 2026](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/09/anthropic-released-claude-fable-5-its-most-powerful-model-publicly-days-after-warning-ai-is-getting-too-dangerous/) `[via snippet]`;
[Anthropic, "When AI builds itself," 4 Jun 2026](https://www.anthropic.com/institute/recursive-self-improvement) `[via snippet]`)

## 📊 Benchmarks & evaluations

**Fable 5 / Mythos 5 benchmark claims — strong on paper, but vendor-reported and
not yet independently verified.** `[vendor PR]` Anthropic's headline numbers:

- **SWE-bench Pro: 80.3%.** SWE-bench is the standard "can the model fix real GitHub
  issues" benchmark — the model is dropped into an actual open-source repo with a bug
  report and graded on whether its patch passes the project's tests; "Pro" is the
  harder, contamination-resistant variant. 80.3% is a large jump: Anthropic cites
  **GPT-5.5 at 58.6%** on the same test. A 22-point gap on SWE-bench Pro is not
  chart-padding — it's the difference between "usually needs a human to finish" and
  "often lands the fix unaided."
- **Cognition FrontierCode Diamond: 29.3%.** This is a newer, deliberately brutal
  *agentic* coding benchmark (the model plans and executes multi-step work across a
  codebase, not just single-file edits) that grades for *maintainable, high-quality*
  output rather than just a passing test. Anthropic reports **29.3% for Fable 5/Mythos
  5 vs 13.4% for Claude Opus 4.8 and 5.7% for GPT-5.5** — roughly a doubling over its
  own prior best. The low absolute numbers are the point: this benchmark is designed so
  that even frontier models mostly fail, which is what makes a 2× move notable.

The honest caveat: these are the lab's own figures, released alongside the model.
Independent runs (the SWE-bench Verified leaderboard, LMArena, Artificial Analysis)
hadn't posted verified Fable 5 numbers as of this writing. Context from before today's
GA: the Mythos *Preview* had already topped the provisional coding leaderboard at a
weighted 100.0%, ahead of Opus 4.8 (98.9%) and Gemini 3.1 Pro (93.6%) — so a
top-of-leaderboard result here would surprise no one, but "trust the vendor slide until
the independent eval lands" applies as always.
([VentureBeat, 9 Jun 2026](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-brings-mythos-to-the-masses-with-claude-fable-5-its-most-powerful-generally-available-model-ever) `[via snippet]`;

## 🍎 Apple Silicon / on-device

A genuinely quiet day here — the depth coverage lives in the Weekend brief — but two
small things worth a local-inference user's attention:

- **LiquidAI LFM2.5-1.2B-JP ships with full MLX + GGUF port set.** LiquidAI's
  **LFM2.5-1.2B-JP** — a 1.2-billion-parameter Japanese/English *edge* model (small
  enough to run on a phone or laptop, not a datacenter GPU) — now has the complete
  on-device port set on Hugging Face: **4-, 5-, 6- and 8-bit MLX** quantizations (MLX is
  Apple's own array framework, the fastest path on Apple Silicon) plus a **GGUF** build
  (the llama.cpp/LM Studio format). If you keep a small always-on local model for
  Japanese text, this is a tidy drop-in for an M-series Mac. ([Hugging Face — LiquidAI/LFM2.5-1.2B-JP-202606](https://hf.co/LiquidAI/LFM2.5-1.2B-JP-202606))

- **Runtime context (from earlier this week, not today):** **LM Studio 0.4.15** (4 Jun)
  bundled **mlx-engine v1.8.1**, adding parallel predictions for vision models (Qwen
  3.5/3.6, Gemma 4) and tensor-parallel multi-GPU loading on the llama.cpp backend. One
  to watch if you track raw throughput: an open llama.cpp issue reports a ~13%
  token-generation regression on **M3 Ultra** between March and current builds. ([codersera — local AI runtimes update, May 2026](https://codersera.com/blog/local-ai-runtimes-may-2026-update/) `[via snippet]`)
