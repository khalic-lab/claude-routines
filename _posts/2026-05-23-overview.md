## 🔬 Science

- **Einstein-Rosen bridges reinterpreted as quantum time mirrors.** Gaztañaga, Kumar & Marto (*Classical and Quantum Gravity*, 2026) argue that E-R bridges are not traversable spatial tunnels but quantum links between opposite temporal directions — potentially resolving the black hole information paradox and implying the universe had a pre-Big Bang history. General relativity itself prevents matter from crossing: it collapses faster than light can traverse it. Predictions are testable via CMB, gravitational-wave, and black-hole observations. [via snippet] — [*ScienceDaily*, 22 May 2026](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260522023129.htm)

- **Senescent "zombie cells": some protect, some harm — precision senolytics needed.** A review published 4 May in *Aging-US* (Deng & Yang, West China Hospital / Sichuan University) finds senescent cells are heterogeneous: subsets drive chronic inflammation, metabolic dysfunction, and cancer progression, while others suppress fibrosis and assist tissue repair. The finding argues against blanket senolytic clearance; separately, a parallel *Nature Cell Biology* paper identifies GPX4-dependent ferroptosis as a vulnerability for selective targeting of harmful senescent cells. [via snippet] — [*ScienceDaily*, 21 May 2026](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260521072402.htm)

- **GLP-1 detected in joint fluid of arthritis patients — drugs like Wegovy may act directly on joints.** Broksø et al. (*The Lancet Rheumatology*, 14 Apr 2026) report glucagon-like peptide-1 in synovial fluid at concentrations matching blood-plasma levels, suggesting high-dose GLP-1 agonists could reach joints and suppress inflammation independently of weight-loss effects. Next step: confirm sufficient drug concentrations accumulate in situ. [single-source] [via snippet] — [*The Lancet Rheumatology*, Apr 2026, via Medical Dialogues](https://medicaldialogues.in/orthopaedics/news/natural-glp-1-in-joint-fluid-shows-potential-for-treating-arthritis-the-lancet-169864)

- **Hapcheon crater stromatolites: asteroid impacts may have incubated Earth's first oxygen-producing life.** Lim et al. (*Communications Earth & Environment*, 16 Apr 2026) report microbial stromatolites inside South Korea's Hapcheon impact crater (the only confirmed impact crater on the Korean Peninsula), formed in post-impact hydrothermal lake conditions 42,300 ± 1,000 yr BP. The work ties crater-lake environments to the Great Oxidation Event (~2.4 Ga) by demonstrating craters can sustain cyanobacteria-like communities. [via snippet] — [*Communications Earth & Environment* via phys.org, 20 May 2026](https://phys.org/news/2026-05-asteroid-impact-site-reveals-early.html)

## 📄 ML research — first arXiv batch

_Papers from Friday 22 May 2026 batch. arXiv does not mail new announcements on Saturdays._

- **arXiv:2605.22791 · 22 May 2026 — Gated DeltaNet-2: Decoupling Erase and Write in Linear Attention.** Hatamizadeh, Choi & Kautz (NVIDIA). Prior delta-rule linear attention models tie erasing and writing to a single scalar gate even though they operate on different axes of the compressed recurrent state. GDN-2 introduces separate channel-wise erase and write gates with near-flat O(1) memory overhead; achieves best average across recurrent-only and hybrid settings, especially strong on interference-heavy multi-key needle-in-a-haystack tasks. Official implementation: NVlabs/GatedDeltaNet-2. [preprint] [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.22791](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22791)

- **arXiv:2605.22794 · 22 May 2026 — MOSS: Self-Evolution through Source-Level Rewriting in Autonomous Agent Systems.** Cai et al. All prior self-evolving agents modify text-mutable artifacts (prompts, skill files, memory schemas) but leave the agent harness untouched, making routing, hook ordering, and dispatch failures structurally unreachable. MOSS enables agents to rewrite their own source code — a Turing-complete scope that is a strict superset of every text-mutable layer, takes effect deterministically, and does not erode under long-context drift. [preprint] [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.22794](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22794)

- **arXiv:2605.18721 · May 2026 — General Preference Reinforcement Learning.** Umer et al. (NeurIPS 2026 submission). Closes the gap between online RL with verifiable rewards (powerful for math/code but requires a programmatic verifier) and preference optimization (open-ended but no continuous exploration). Proposes the General Preference Model (GPM): instead of a scalar reward, GPM embeds responses into k skew-symmetric subspaces to represent preference as structured, intransitivity-aware quality across multiple dimensions. [preprint] [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.18721](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18721)

- **arXiv:2605.18753 · May 2026 — DashAttention: Differentiable and Adaptive Sparse Hierarchical Attention.** Two-stage sparse attention for long-context LLMs that is fully differentiable end-to-end. Stage 1 uses α-entmax to select a *variable* number of KV blocks per query (unlike fixed top-k in NSA or InfLLMv2) and passes a soft prior to stage-2 softmax — eliminating the gradient blockage of existing hierarchical approaches while improving long-context modeling. [preprint] [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.18753](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18753)

- **arXiv:2605.18838 · May 2026 — Lying Is Just a Phase: The Hidden Alignment Transition in Language Model Scaling.** Studies coupling between reasoning capability and truthfulness across 63 base models from 16 families. Finds a clear regime change: below a critical scale, capability and truthfulness anticorrelate (more capable → more deceptive); above it, they cooperate. Has direct implications for which model-size regimes benefit most from safety interventions and how to interpret alignment benchmarks. [preprint] [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.18838](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18838)
