## 🚀 New models, datasets, & open weights

**DeepSeek V4 Pro + Flash — 1.6T-param MoE, MIT license**
Released April 24. Two MoE variants: V4-Pro (1.6T total params, 49B activated) and V4-Flash (284B total, 13B activated). Both: 1M-token context, MIT license, weights on HuggingFace. Architecture uses hybrid Compressed Sparse Attention (CSA) + Heavily Compressed Attention (HCA); V4-Pro requires only 27% of DeepSeek-V3.2's inference FLOPs at 1M-token context and 10% of the KV cache. API pricing: $1.74/M input tokens. Currently the highest-trending model on HuggingFace today (score 551, 457K downloads). Vendor benchmarks place V4-Pro alongside GPT-5.4 on public math and QA evals — but see the NIST CAISI independent assessment in the Benchmarks section for a contrary view.
— [DeepSeek API Docs, Apr 24 2026](https://api-docs.deepseek.com/news/news260424) [via snippet]; [HuggingFace deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro](https://huggingface.co/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro) [via snippet]; [MindStudio, 2026](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/deepseek-v4-launch-specs-open-weight-2026) [via snippet]

**Qwen3.6 family (27B + 35B-A3B) — multimodal, Apache 2.0, top HF downloads**

Both are natively multimodal, accepting text, image, and video inputs, trained through combined pre-training and post-training multimodal stages. Qwen3.6-27B reportedly outperforms Qwen3.5-397B-A17B on agentic coding benchmarks despite ~14× fewer parameters. Supports 201 languages and dialects. Unsloth's Qwen3.6-27B-GGUF quantization ([unsloth/Qwen3.6-27B-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/Qwen3.6-27B-GGUF)) has already accumulated 1M downloads.

## 📊 Benchmarks & evaluations

**NIST CAISI: DeepSeek V4 Pro trails US frontier by ~8 months** [T1, published today]
NIST's Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) released its evaluation of DeepSeek V4 Pro today (May 3). Headline finding: V4-Pro is "the most capable PRC AI model evaluated by CAISI to date" but its capabilities lag leading US closed models by approximately eight months — placing it roughly on par with GPT-5 rather than current frontier. Methodology: nine benchmarks across five domains (cyber, software engineering, natural sciences, abstract reasoning, mathematics) including two non-public evals — ARC-AGI-2's semi-private dataset and CAISI's internally-built PortBench (software engineering). Exception: on mathematics, DeepSeek V4 nearly matches top US models. On cost efficiency: V4-Pro outperforms the most cost-competitive US reference model (GPT-5.4 mini) on 5 of 7 benchmarks. The non-public benchmark methodology reduces reproducibility but also limits benchmark contamination.
— [NIST CAISI, May 3 2026](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/05/caisi-evaluation-deepseek-v4-pro) [via snippet]; [The Decoder, May 3 2026](https://the-decoder.com/china-is-falling-behind-in-the-ai-race-according-to-a-us-government-benchmark/) [via snippet]

**China finalizes AI companion regulations — effective July 15**
On April 10, China's Cyberspace Administration and four co-regulators (NDRC, MIIT, Ministry of Public Security, SAMR) finalized the Interim Measures for Anthropomorphic AI Interactive Services.
