## 🔬 Science

- **Oxford achieves first quadsqueezing: a fourth-order quantum squeezing interaction, 100× faster than predicted.** By combining two non-commuting forces on a single trapped ion, the team generated trisqueezing and quadsqueezing interactions — long theorized but never demonstrated — via the non-commutativity of quantum operations. Applications: quantum sensing, simulation, computing. Published in *Nature Physics* May 1. [*Nature Physics*, May 1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03222-6); [ScienceDaily, May 1](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260501052828.htm)

- **TSCM CAR-T trial: 5 of 11 blood cancer patients in complete remission — with no chemotherapy preconditioning.** Stem-cell memory T cells (T_SCM), a rare self-renewing immune subset, achieved complete responses at low doses without lymphodepletion, which normally precedes CAR-T infusions. One further patient had partial remission. Trial is small; larger studies underway. Published in *Cell* (Apr 30). [Nature news, May 1](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01398-8); [EurekAlert](https://www.eurekalert.org/news-releases/1125973)

- **LHCb "penguin" B-meson decay anomaly strengthens against Standard Model predictions.** When a B meson decays to a strange kaon and two muons, the angular distributions diverge from SM expectations — and the deviation has grown, not shrunk, with more LHCb data. Accepted for *Physical Review Letters*; described as "one of the last remaining anomalies" in particle physics. Caution: prior anomalies (e.g., W-boson mass) have evaporated under scrutiny. [Nature.com news, May 1](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01387-x) [single-source at T2 level; primary: PRL accepted manuscript]

- **"Menstruating" endometrial organoids reveal how uterine lining repairs without scarring.** The 3D mini-tissues shed and regenerate in vitro, implicating luminal epithelial cells — not just glandular cells — in the mystery of monthly scarless repair. Could inform endometriosis and implantation research. Published in *Cell Stem Cell* (Apr 28). [Nature.com news](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01428-5) [single-source at T2 level; primary: Cell Stem Cell paper]
