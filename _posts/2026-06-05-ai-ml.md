## 🏢 Lab blogs & official releases

- **Google ships Gemma 4 12B — a "laptop-class" multimodal open model that hears.** `[vendor PR]`
  Google released **Gemma 4 12B** (12-billion-parameter open weights, Apache 2.0 license) on June 3, the
  mid-size addition between the existing edge-friendly Gemma 4 E4B and the 26B Mixture-of-Experts (MoE —
  an architecture where only a fraction of the network "fires" per token, so a big model runs cheaply).
  The headline is an **encoder-free** design: rather than bolting a separate audio encoder onto the
  language model, Gemma 4 12B projects the raw audio waveform directly into the same vector space as text
  tokens, making it the first medium-size open model to natively ingest audio (alongside text and image).
  It targets a 256K-token context, 140+ languages, and — the part that matters for local use — runs on a
  **16 GB laptop**. Google claims it beats the older Gemma 3 27B on GPQA Diamond (a hard graduate-level
  science Q&A set), MMLU-Pro, and others at under half the size. Why it matters: capable multimodal
  inference is sliding down from datacenters onto consumer hardware, and "encoder-free audio" is a real
  architectural simplification, not just a checkpoint bump. — [Google blog, 2026-06-03](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/introducing-gemma-4-12b/) `[vendor PR]` `[via snippet]`

## 🚀 New models, datasets & open weights

- **Ideogram 4.0 — a 9.3B open-weight image model that's actually good at text-in-images.** Ideogram
  released **Ideogram 4.0** on June 3: a 9.3-billion-parameter single-stream **Diffusion Transformer**
  (the now-standard image-generator architecture — a transformer that iteratively denoises random noise
  into a picture) trained from scratch and paired with a vision-language text encoder. Standout features:
  native **2K resolution**, transparent backgrounds, precise layout control via bounding boxes, structured
  **JSON prompting** (you specify text placement, color, and layout as fields rather than hoping the prose
  prompt is obeyed), and notably strong in-image text rendering — historically the weakest spot for image
  models. **License caveat:** the inference *code* is Apache 2.0, but the *weights* sit under Ideogram's
  Non-Commercial Model Agreement — free to download, fine-tune, and run for research, but commercial use
  needs a paid license, so "open weights" here means open-for-research, not fully open. It ranks #1 among
  open-weight models on DesignArena (a head-to-head design-quality leaderboard) at ~1285 Elo, a claimed
  ~115-point lead over the next open competitor. Available via Ideogram's API and on Hugging Face,
  ComfyUI, fal, Replicate, and others. — [Ideogram, 2026-06-03](https://ideogram.ai/news/ideogram-4.0/) · [The Decoder, 2026-06-03](https://the-decoder.com/ideogram-4-0-drops-as-an-open-weight-model-with-native-2k-resolution-and-improved-text-rendering/) `[via snippet]`

- **Holo3.1 — open computer-use agents that run locally at 140 ms.** French startup **H Company**
  released **Holo3.1** on June 2: an open-weights (Apache 2.0) family of **computer-use** models — systems
  that drive a real GUI by looking at the screen and issuing clicks/keystrokes, the substrate for "do this
  task in my browser" agents. Sizes span 0.8B to a **35B-A3B** MoE (35B total parameters, ~3B active per
  token). The pitch is *local and fast*: ~140 ms per-step latency and **74.2% on OSWorld** (a benchmark of
  real desktop tasks across apps), with the 35B variant jumping AndroidWorld from 67% → 79.3%. Crucially
  for on-device use it ships in **FP8, Q4 GGUF, and NVFP4** quantized formats — the GGUF build slots
  straight into the llama.cpp/local-inference stack, and the quantized checkpoints lose only ~2 OSWorld
  points versus full precision. Why it matters: usable computer-use agents that don't have to round-trip
  every screenshot to a cloud API — better latency, and the screen contents never leave the machine.
  — [H Company on Hugging Face, 2026-06-02](https://huggingface.co/blog/Hcompany/holo31) · [model card](https://huggingface.co/Hcompany/Holo-3.1-35B-A3B) `[via snippet]`

- **Gemma 4 12B weights** (see §Lab blogs) are downloadable on Hugging Face, Kaggle, and via Ollama on
  day one, including quantized builds for local runners — see the Apple Silicon section below.

## 📊 Benchmarks & evaluations

- **LMArena launches Agent Arena — the first leaderboard scored from real, in-the-wild agent behavior.**
  On June 4, LMArena (arena.ai) launched **Agent Arena**, a leaderboard for *agentic* performance rather
  than single-turn chat quality. The methodological novelty is the scoring: instead of pairwise human
  preference votes (LMArena's classic A/B "which answer is better" approach), Agent Arena aggregates
  **live behavioral signals** from hundreds of thousands of real agent sessions — explicit task-success
  labels, turn-by-turn natural-language feedback, artifact-download events (did the user actually keep the
  file the agent produced?), retries, and disapproval events. It ranks models with **causal tracing** — a
  technique borrowed from causal inference that treats the agent as a multi-component system and asks which
  component choice actually *caused* a good or bad outcome, rather than just correlating. Why it matters:
  static benchmarks (SWE-bench, etc.) and preference votes both miss whether an agent is *useful over a
  long real task*; building the eval from production telemetry is a meaningfully different — and harder to
  game — signal. Caveat: telemetry-based ranking inherits whatever biases live in who uses arena.ai/agent
  and for what. — [Agent Arena, LMArena, 2026-06-04](https://arena.ai/blog/agent-arena/) · [methodology](https://arena.ai/blog/agent-arena-methodology/) `[via snippet]`

- **Eval-landscape context (not new today, for calibration).** Where the frontier sits on the
  most-cited benchmarks, per current public leaderboards: **Claude Opus 4.8** leads SWE-bench Verified
  (the human-audited subset of real GitHub bug-fix tasks) at ~88.6%, with GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro well
  behind on that specific test; on the harder **SWE-bench Pro**, MiniMax M3's vendor-run 59% edged GPT-5.5
  but trails Opus. By contrast, on **ARC-AGI-3** (an abstract-reasoning benchmark of novel interactive
  puzzles designed to resist memorization) frontier systems still score **under 1%** where humans solve
  ~100% — a standing reminder that "tops the coding chart" and "general reasoning" are very different axes.
  Read leaderboard deltas with the methodology in mind: a 2-point SWE-bench move is real; a vendor-run,
  pre-release number is a press release. — [llm-stats SWE-bench Verified](https://llm-stats.com/benchmarks/swe-bench-verified) · [ARC-AGI-3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24621) `[via snippet]` `[single-source]`

## 💼 Industry, funding, regulation

The week's through-line: AI megarounds are concentrating in *physical* and *vertical* AI, not chatbots.

- **Generalist AI raises $400M at a $2B valuation to build robot foundation models.** **Generalist AI**
  — founded by veterans of OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and Boston Dynamics (CEO Pete Florence) — raised $400M
  led by **Radical Ventures**, with 8VC, Union Square Ventures, Norwest, Hanabi, and existing backers
  **Nvidia** and **Bezos Expeditions**, valuing the company at ~$2B. The thesis is "physical AGI": a single
  *foundation model* (one model trained broadly, then adapted) that controls many different robot bodies,
  rather than bespoke software per robot. Its **GEN-1** model (launched April 2026) claims 99% reliability
  across dexterous tasks and up to 3× faster execution than prior state-of-the-art. Capital goes to scaling
  models, real-world data collection, and compute. Why it matters: the "brain for every robot" bet is now
  one of the most heavily funded theses in AI, and Nvidia + Bezos co-signing it is a strong signal.
  — [Bloomberg, 2026-06-04](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-04/nvidia-backed-robotics-startup-generalist-ai-valued-at-2-billion) · [The Robot Report, 2026-06-04](https://www.therobotreport.com/generalist-raises-400m-to-scale-its-general-purpose-ai-models/) `[via snippet]`

- **Suno raises $400M+ Series D at a $5.4B valuation — while still being sued by the labels.** AI music
  generator **Suno** closed $400M+ led by **Bond Capital** (with IVP, Forerunner, Union Square Ventures,
  Alkeon, Quiet, plus returning Matrix/Lightspeed/Menlo/Schroders), announced June 3. The round more than
  **doubles** its valuation from $2.45B seven months ago to $5.4B. Traction: 2M+ paying subscribers, a
  reported 7M songs generated daily, and top-3 in Apple's App Store music category in several countries.
  The legal cloud is the story's other half: Suno settled with Warner Music in November but remains in
  active copyright litigation with **Universal** and **Sony**, plus Denmark's Koda and Germany's GEMA.
  Why it matters: investors are pricing AI music as a winner despite unresolved training-data copyright
  liability — the same tension running through the broader generative-AI legal docket.
  — [TechCrunch, 2026-06-03](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/03/still-facing-copyright-lawsuits-ai-music-generator-suno-raises-another-400m/) · [Music Business Worldwide, 2026-06-03](https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/suno-raises-over-400-million-pushing-valuation-to-5-4-billion/) `[via snippet]`

- **NewLimit raises $435M Series C at $3.1B for AI-guided longevity science.** **NewLimit** — co-founded
  by Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong, stem-cell biologist Jacob Kimmel (CEO), and Blake Byers — closed $435M
  led by **Founders Fund**, with Thrive, Greenoaks, Quiet, and returning Kleiner Perkins, Eli Lilly
  Ventures, and Nat Friedman/Daniel Gross, tripling its valuation to $3.1B. The science: **epigenetic
  reprogramming** — altering the chemical "markings" on DNA that decide which genes a cell uses, to coax
  old cells back toward a youthful state (the genome itself is unchanged; only its regulation is). NewLimit
  says it reversed cell age in old *human liver cells* and aims for first human trials next year. The AI
  angle: machine-learning models search the vast combinatorial space of reprogramming factors to find
  combinations that rejuvenate without dedifferentiating cells into a cancer-risk state. Why it matters: a
  marquee example of AI-as-discovery-engine in biology pulling a nine-figure round — and a real clinical
  timeline, not just a platform pitch. — [NewLimit blog, 2026-06-02](https://blog.newlimit.com/p/newlimit-raises-435m-led-by-founders) · [BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/business/newlimit-snags-435m-after-seeing-age-reversal-in-human-liver-cells) `[via snippet]`

- **Apoha exits stealth with $36M for "liquid intelligence" — teaching AI how matter behaves.**
  London/San-Francisco deep-tech startup **Apoha** emerged from stealth with $36M (a 2024 seed plus a
  spring round, led by **Singular**, with Draper Associates and seed backers Redalpine, Seedcamp, Wilbe,
  Nucleus). The idea is a genuinely different data modality: rather than training on text or images, Apoha
  measures how a tiny material sample (pinhead-scale, suspended in liquid) *physically responds* to a
  controlled series of stresses, capturing the wave patterns it emits — its product **VIBE** (Variations
  in Inter-facial Behaviour Under Excitation). The bet is that this "empirical readout" of molecular
  behavior trains models to predict how new substances — proteins, foods, paints, drug formulations —
  will act in the real world. Early commercial validation: with Boehringer Ingelheim it flagged high-risk
  antibody candidates at >90% precision from as little as 8 µg of material; further work with Ethris
  (mRNA lipid nanoparticles) and food startup THIS. Why it matters: a reminder that "AI" progress can come
  from a *new sensor and dataset*, not just bigger language models. — [Fortune, 2026-06-03](https://fortune.com/2026/06/03/apoha-36-million-series-a-funding-round-exit-stealth-wave-form-ai-liquid-intelligence/) · [Tech.eu, 2026-06-03](https://tech.eu/2026/06/03/apoha-emerges-from-stealth-with-36m-to-build-liquid-state-intelligence-for-molecular-behaviour/) `[via snippet]`

- **Trump signs the frontier-AI executive order: a classified cyber-capability benchmark and voluntary
  pre-release access.** The "pre-release AI evaluation" framework floated in May is now signed. On June 2,
  President Trump signed **"Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security,"** which
  directs Treasury, NSA (via DoD), and CISA (via DHS) to build — within 60 days — a **classified
  benchmarking process** to assess AI models' offensive *cyber* capabilities and set a secret threshold
  for designating a model a **"covered frontier model"** (the NSA director makes the call; the criteria
  stay classified). Crucially the regime is **voluntary**: developers are *invited* to give the government
  up to **30 days of pre-release access** to evaluate covered models, with IP/cybersecurity protections —
  but public release is **not** conditioned on government sign-off, and there's no statutory enforcement.
  Why it matters: it's the most concrete US federal posture yet on frontier models, but its "carrots, not
  mandates" design means its bite depends entirely on whether labs opt in — and the secret criteria make
  outside accountability hard. — [White House, 2026-06-02](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/) · [WilmerHale analysis](https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/20260602-new-executive-order-addressing-early-government-access-to-frontier-ai-models) `[via snippet]` `[ongoing since 2026-05-22]`
