**OpenAI updates GPT-Rosalind, its life-sciences model, and opens the preview worldwide.**
`[vendor PR]` OpenAI shipped a new version of GPT-Rosalind — a model purpose-built for drug
discovery and quantitative biology rather than general chat — and, for the first time, opened the
research preview to eligible organizations globally through a vetted ("trusted-access") deployment.
OpenAI's own numbers claim it beats GPT-5.5 (their general-purpose frontier model) across tested

a cheaper-per-task specialist that biotech labs can actually run, and the global-access expansion

## 🚀 New models, datasets, & open weights

**NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Ultra — the 550B open weights are now actually live, plus day-one community
quants.** `[ongoing since 2026-06-03]` Yesterday this was a "drops June 4" teaser; today the weights
are real. NVIDIA published the full family on Hugging Face: the instruct model
[`NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Ultra-550B-A55B-BF16`](https://hf.co/nvidia/NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Ultra-550B-A55B-BF16)
(560B total parameters, ~55B active per token — a sparse Mixture-of-Experts, or MoE, where only a
fraction of the network fires on each token to save compute), a matching **base** checkpoint, and an
**NVFP4** quantized variant (NVIDIA's 4-bit format that shrinks the memory footprint for serving).
The architecture is `nemotron_h` — a **hybrid Mamba-Transformer** (Mamba/SSM layers are a
state-space alternative to attention that scales linearly with sequence length, cheaper for very
long context) with **MTP** (multi-token prediction, decoding several tokens per step for speed).
License is the NVIDIA Open Model License (commercial use permitted), training data the public
`nemotron-post-training-v3` set. Within hours an
[unsloth GGUF port](https://hf.co/unsloth/NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Ultra-550B-A55B-GGUF) appeared
(GGUF = the llama.cpp weight format for local/CPU inference). Why it matters: this is the strongest
open-weights model a US lab has shipped, and the same-day GGUF/NVFP4 work means people can start
running it on their own hardware immediately rather than waiting weeks. (Benchmark positioning is in
the next section.)
[Hugging Face model card](https://hf.co/nvidia/NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Ultra-550B-A55B-BF16)

## 📊 Benchmarks & evaluations

**Where Nemotron 3 Ultra actually lands: best US open weights, still behind China.** On the
Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index (a composite score blending reasoning, knowledge and coding
evals into a single number — useful for rough ranking, not a substitute for task-specific testing),
Nemotron 3 Ultra reportedly scores **48**, comfortably ahead of the next US open-weights models
(Gemma 4 31B ~39, gpt-oss-120b ~33) but trailing the top Chinese open models such as Kimi K2.6
(~54). The honest read: the US open-weights gap to China narrowed but did not close. Methodology
caveat — composite indices hide which sub-skills moved, so "48 vs 54" tells you ranking, not where
the six points actually are. [Decrypt, 2026-06](https://decrypt.co/369689/nvidia-open-ai-model-nemotron-3-ultra) `[via snippet]`

**MiniMax M3 claims the top open-weight SWE-Bench Pro score — but it's vendor-run and pre-release.**
Shanghai's MiniMax says M3 hits **59.0% on SWE-Bench Pro** (a harder variant of SWE-Bench, the
benchmark that makes a model resolve real GitHub issues by editing a repo and passing the project's
tests — a far better proxy for "can it actually code" than trivia evals), edging past Kimi K2.6's
58.6% and, it claims, several closed APIs. Why the asterisk: the numbers are MiniMax's own, the open
weights had not shipped at the time of the claim (reportedly ~June 11), so nobody outside the lab has
verified them yet. `[disputed]` "Beats GPT-5.5 on SWE-Bench Pro" is only as good as the harness they
ran it in. [The Decoder, 2026-06](https://the-decoder.com/minimax-m3-open-weight-model-with-a-million-token-context-challenges-proprietary-leaders/) `[via snippet]`

**Frontier coding leaderboard, for context.** On SWE-Bench Verified (the human-audited subset that's
become the de-facto coding gold standard), the public leaderboard this week shows Anthropic's
internal "Claude Mythos Preview" out front at ~93.9%, with Claude Opus 4.8 at ~88.6% — i.e. the
verified-coding frontier is still a closed-model affair, and open weights like Nemotron/M3 are
competing a clear tier below it. [BenchLM, 2026-06](https://benchlm.ai/benchmarks/sweVerified) `[via snippet]`

## 🍎 Apple Silicon / on-device

**Nemotron 3 Ultra reaches the local-inference stack same-day.** Beyond the official NVFP4 build, the
community
[unsloth GGUF port](https://hf.co/unsloth/NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Ultra-550B-A55B-GGUF) (created June 4)
makes the 550B model loadable by llama.cpp — though at 550B total it's a multi-GPU / high-RAM
proposition, not a laptop model. Relevant plumbing: recent llama.cpp work added a **CUDA fusion for
SSM_CONV + ADD(bias) + SILU**, which speeds up exactly the Mamba/state-space layers that
Nemotron-H-style hybrids use (fusing several small ops into one kernel cuts memory traffic). On
Apple Silicon the Metal path lags the CUDA path for these hybrid kernels, so Mac users will likely
wait for ports — the practical near-term Mac story is the smaller Nemotron 3 Nano variants, not
Ultra. [single-source] [llama.cpp discussion #20421](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/discussions/20421) `[via snippet]`
