## 🔬 Science

- **A pan-RAS inhibitor finally drugs the "undruggable" in pancreatic cancer.** In the RASolute 302 trial, daraxonrasib — a drug that disables all three members of the RAS family (signalling proteins whose mutations drive >90% of pancreatic cancers and were considered chemically untouchable for ~40 years) — roughly **doubled** the time tumours stayed in check versus standard chemotherapy. Results were presented at the ASCO oncology meeting and published simultaneously in NEJM; oncologists are calling it one of the most anticipated cancer readouts in years. Why it matters: pancreatic cancer is among the deadliest solid tumours, and a working RAS drug reopens a target that underlies many other cancers too. [Nature news, 2026-06-01](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01760-w); corroborated [AJMC, 2026-06-01 [via snippet]](https://www.ajmc.com/view/rasolute-302-brings-a-transformative-moment-on-pancreatic-cancer-a-60-improvement-in-overall-survival)
- **Gravitational waves confirm a "forbidden zone" in black-hole masses — and weigh in on nuclear physics.** Analysing the catalogue of black-hole merger signals, astronomers confirm a **pair-instability mass gap** (theory says stars between ~50 and ~130 solar masses blow themselves apart entirely, leaving no black hole — so few black holes should exist in that band). Black holes seen *above* the gap therefore weren't born directly from a collapsing star but built up by repeated mergers in dense star clusters. Crucially, the gap's lower edge is set by the carbon-to-oxygen fusion rate (the `12C(α,γ)16O` reaction) inside massive stars, so the gravitational-wave data feeds back a constraint on a notoriously hard-to-measure nuclear reaction. [Nature Astronomy, 2026-06-01](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41550-026-02856-z); underlying analysis [arXiv:2509.04637 [preprint]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.04637), reporting via [phys.org, 2026-05 [via snippet]](https://phys.org/news/2026-05-monster-black-holes-usual-history.html)
- **Neptune's moon Triton may still run a magnetic dynamo — leftover heat from being kidnapped.** Triton orbits Neptune backwards, the tell-tale sign it's a captured Kuiper-belt body rather than a native moon. New modelling argues that the violent capture and orbit-circularisation heated Triton enough to melt out a metallic core; convection in that core could power an active **dynamo** (the churning-liquid-metal engine that makes a planetary magnetic field) producing ~1 microtesla at the surface today. If true, a future flyby's magnetometer would have to disentangle this dynamo from signals of a suspected subsurface ocean. [single-source] [Nature Astronomy, 2026-06-01](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41550-026-02868-9)
- **Sterilised soil kept doing life-like chemistry for nearly seven years.** A French INRAE team report that soil with no living microbes kept releasing CO₂ — and sped up when fed glucose — for 2,442 days, implying metals and minerals can catalyse metabolism-like reactions on their own. It's evidence for the "metabolism-first" camp in origin-of-life research, which holds that the chemical cycles of life ran on geology *before* enzymes and genes existed to control them. [Quanta, 2026-06-01](https://www.quantamagazine.org/the-dirt-that-refused-to-die-20260601/)
- **Gene therapy that turns a brain tumour into its own interferon factory: early human data.** A phase 1 trial delivered a gene for interferon-α (an immune-signalling protein that recruits the body's anti-tumour defences) directly into glioblastoma — the most aggressive and near-uniformly fatal brain cancer — aiming to manufacture the drug locally instead of dosing the whole body. Phase 1 reads out mainly safety and dosing, not a cure, but local delivery is the key idea being tested. [single-source] [preprint→trial] [Nature Medicine, 2026-06-01](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04419-1)
- **2,520 diners vindicate Feynman's 50-year-old "restaurant dilemma" math.** Feynman once turned "order my favourite or gamble on a new dish?" into a clean **explore-vs-exploit** problem (keep trying new options until one clears a quality threshold, then settle). A new study reproduced his answer, solved a generalised version, and found that real people don't compute the optimal rule but use a simpler heuristic that scores nearly as well. Why it matters: explore-vs-exploit is the same math behind A/B testing, clinical-trial design and reinforcement learning. [Nature news, 2026-06-01](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00821-4); study in PNAS via [Science News, 2026 [via snippet]](https://www.sciencenews.org/article/math-restaurant-meal-feynman)

## 📄 ML research — first arXiv batch

_First cs.LG / cs.AI / cs.CL / stat.ML submissions surfacing this morning (UTC). All [preprint]._

- **BitsMoE — `arXiv:2606.00079` (2026-06-02).** Squeezes Mixture-of-Experts LLMs (models that route each token to a few specialist sub-networks but must still keep *all* experts in memory) into the ultra-low-bit range by allocating precision per-expert via a spectral-energy importance score, instead of crudely quantising everything alike. Why interesting: MoE deployment is memory-bound, so smarter bit budgets directly cut the GPU footprint. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00079)
- **BudgetDraft — `arXiv:2606.00144` (2026-06-02).** Fixes a known failure of speculative decoding (where a small "drafter" model guesses several tokens and a big "verifier" checks them in parallel to go faster) when the drafter runs a trimmed **KV-cache** — the stored attention keys/values — to save memory. Acceptance-aware training keeps the draft accepted at 4K–16K context, where the naive sparse/full mismatch otherwise tanks the speed-up. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00144)
- **ReMax — `arXiv:2606.00151` (2026-06-02).** Proposes an RL objective that scores a policy by its *expected best-of-M* return rather than its average, and shows exploration then emerges on its own — no hand-tuned bonus terms. Why interesting: it gives a principled reason agents should explore (you only benefit from trying alternatives when you'll revisit similar states), relevant to current best-of-N inference and RL-for-LLMs work. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00151)
- **TIGER — `arXiv:2606.00232` (2026-06-02).** Tackles hallucination in multimodal generation (fluent output stating "facts" the image/input doesn't support) by extracting claims independently and routing fact-level evidence for targeted repair, rather than rewriting from the contaminated output. Why interesting: fact-level, rankable feedback is more auditable than free-form self-correction. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00232)
- **DLLM-JEPA — `arXiv:2606.00091` (2026-06-02).** Ports JEPA (Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture — self-supervised learning that predicts in representation space rather than reconstructing raw tokens) onto masked-diffusion language models. The diffusion model's bidirectional attention supplies two distinct "views" for free, dropping JEPA's usual need for paired data and a second forward pass. Why interesting: a concrete hybrid of two non-autoregressive paradigms aimed at cheaper representation learning for text. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00091)
- **Agentic tool-calling is fragile to undocumented knobs — `arXiv:2606.00135` (2026-06-02).** A systematic study finds tool-calling benchmark scores swing on seemingly trivial, often-unreported choices (random seed, system prompt, multi-turn templating), then examines how to train the skill efficiently with RL. Why interesting: a reproducibility caution for the agent-eval leaderboards everyone is currently citing. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00135)
