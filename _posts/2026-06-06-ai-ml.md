What the labs *did* publish were governance and security moves, not capabilities.

- **OpenAI extends ChatGPT "Lockdown Mode" to every personal account.** OpenAI rolled its
  **Lockdown Mode** out to all personal ChatGPT tiers (Free, Go, Plus, Pro) plus self-serve Business
  accounts on June 4 — previously it was limited to Enterprise/Edu/Healthcare since its February
  debut. Lockdown Mode is a deliberately *conservative* setting: when on, it disables or restricts
  the features that let ChatGPT reach the open web or connected external services, which shrinks the
  attack surface for **prompt injection** (an attack where text hidden in a web page or document the
  model reads contains instructions that hijack the model into leaking data or taking unwanted
  actions — the model can't reliably tell "content to summarize" from "commands to obey"). The
  trade-off is explicit: you give up browsing/connectors in exchange for the model being unable to
  exfiltrate your data to an attacker-controlled destination. Why it matters: it's a tacit admission
  from the largest consumer AI vendor that prompt injection remains unsolved at the model layer, so
  the practical defense is to let cautious users turn capabilities *off*. — [OpenAI, 2026](https://openai.com/index/introducing-lockdown-mode-and-elevated-risk-labels-in-chatgpt/) · [Simon Willison, 2026-06-05](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/5/openai-help-lockdown-mode/) `[ongoing since 2026-02-09]`

## 🚀 New models, datasets & open weights

A thin day for *new* drops: yesterday's open-weight wave (Google's Gemma 4 12B, Ideogram 4.0,
H Company's Holo3.1) is already covered, and no comparable fresh release landed today. The one item
that matured into a real release this week:

- **NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Ultra is now actually live — 550B open weights, with the *training data and
  recipe* published too.** `[vendor PR]` Teased at Computex and flagged here on June 3 as "due June 4,"
  Nemotron 3 Ultra went live on Hugging Face, ModelScope, and OpenRouter on **June 4**, and the
  download metadata confirms it: the four official checkpoints (a `BF16` base, a `BF16` post-trained
  instruct model, an `NVFP4` quantized build, and a `GenRM` reward-model variant) are on the Hub, and
  community quantizations were already appearing 24–48 h later. It's a **550-billion-parameter
  Mixture-of-Experts** model (MoE — only a slice of the network, here ~55B parameters, "fires" per
  token, so a very large model runs at a far smaller model's cost) built on a **hybrid
  Mamba-Transformer** backbone (the HF model type is `nemotron_h`; Mamba is a *state-space* sequence
  model that processes long context in linear time instead of the transformer's quadratic
  attention — cheaper memory for long inputs, at some cost to recall). The genuinely unusual part is
  the license: NVIDIA shipped not just weights but the pretraining data blends and post-training
  recipes under the Linux Foundation's permissive **OpenMDW-1.1** — closer to "open *source*" than the
  usual "open *weights*," where you get the binary but not how it was made. Why it matters: a fully
  reproducible 550B model from a US vendor is a real contribution to the open ecosystem, even though
  (see Benchmarks) it still trails the best Chinese open-weight models on aggregate quality. — [NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Ultra model card, Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/nvidia/NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Ultra-550B-A55B-BF16) · [Davarion, 2026-06](https://davarion.com/en/blog/nvidia-nemotron-3-ultra-550b-open-weight-model-computex-2026/) `[ongoing since 2026-06-03]`

- **…and NVIDIA dumped the training corpus alongside it.** Part of the same release: NVIDIA published
  a stack of **Nemotron pretraining and RL datasets** on the Hub (e.g. `Nemotron-RL-Science-v1`,
  `Nemotron-Pretraining-Legal-v1`, `Nemotron-RL-Ultra-Training-Blends`, and an
  `Agentic-Indirect-Prompt-Injection` RL set for hardening tool-use agents against the same injection
  attacks OpenAI's Lockdown Mode mitigates). RLVR here = "reinforcement learning from verifiable
  rewards" — RL where the reward is a checkable fact (the math answer is right, the code compiles)
  rather than a learned human-preference score. For people who fine-tune, having the *data* is often
  more useful than another checkpoint. — [NVIDIA datasets on Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/datasets/nvidia/Nemotron-RL-Ultra-Training-Blends) `[vendor PR]`

## 📊 Benchmarks & evaluations

No new benchmark *methodology* or leaderboard launched today (yesterday's LMArena Agent Arena was the
recent novelty; the SWE-bench Verified frontier — Claude Mythos Preview ~93.9%, Opus 4.8 ~88.6% — is
unchanged and already covered). The one fresh data point worth calibrating:

- **Nemotron 3 Ultra scores 48 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index — best US open-weight
  model to date, but China's best still leads.** Artificial Analysis's **Intelligence Index** is a
  composite that averages a model across a basket of reasoning, knowledge, math, and coding tests into
  a single 0–100-ish number, so it smooths out cherry-picked single-benchmark wins. Nemotron 3 Ultra's
  **48** is, per the index, the highest of any US-built open-weight model — but **Kimi K2.6** (Moonshot
  AI, Beijing) sits at **54**, and DeepSeek/Qwen-class open models also rank above it. Read plainly:
  the open-weight quality frontier is currently held by Chinese labs, and the most newsworthy US
  open-weight release of the week lands a notch behind. NVIDIA's counter-pitch is *speed* — it claims
  Nemotron runs 3–6× faster in tokens/second than comparable Chinese models served via API, which (if
  it holds up in independent testing) matters more for production latency than a 6-point index gap.
  Treat both numbers as vendor/aggregator-reported until independently reproduced. — [The Planet Tools, 2026-06](https://theplanettools.ai/blog/nvidia-nemotron-3-ultra-best-us-open-weights-model-china-still-ahead-june-2026) · [Artificial Analysis (Kimi K2.6 writeup)](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/kimi-k2-6-the-new-leading-open-weights-model) `[single-source]` `[via snippet]`

## 💼 Industry, funding & regulation

The day's two through-lines: the labs banding together on *biosecurity*, and capital still flowing
into AI that isn't a chatbot.

- **OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, and Microsoft AI chiefs jointly ask Congress to mandate
  synthetic-DNA screening.** In a rare show of cross-rival alignment, **Sam Altman, Dario Amodei,
  Demis Hassabis, and Mustafa Suleyman** co-signed an open letter to Congress (June 5) urging a legal
  requirement that all **synthetic-DNA providers** — companies that manufacture made-to-order strands
  of genetic code and ship them to labs — screen what they're being asked to make and verify who's
  ordering. The argument: AI tools are eroding the *expertise barrier* that historically kept
  bioweapon design out of reach, while synthetic DNA can be ordered online with little vetting, so the
  cheapest chokepoint is the DNA supplier, not the model. Signatories include Nobel-laureate
  scientists, DNA-industry executives, and former US defense officials; the letter was organized by the
  Foundation for American Innovation and the Institute for Progress, and it dovetails with the existing
  bipartisan **Biosecurity Modernization and Innovation Act** (Cotton–Klobuchar, Feb 2026). Why it
  matters: it's the labs publicly arguing that *their own* technology lowers a catastrophic-misuse
  barrier — and pointing regulators at a non-AI control point — which is both a genuine safety move and
  a way to shape regulation toward suppliers rather than models. — [Fortune, 2026-06-05](https://fortune.com/2026/06/05/openai-anthropic-microsoft-ceos-congress-bioweapon-safeguards/) · [Yahoo News, 2026-06-05](https://www.yahoo.com/news/politics/articles/openai-anthropic-google-ceos-call-135317808.html) `[via snippet]`

- **Flourish raises $500M at a ~$2.5B valuation to build "brain-efficiency" AI that sips watts.**
  **Flourish** — co-founded by Thomas Reardon (the engineer who built Internet Explorer at Microsoft,
  then founded neural-interface startup CTRL-labs, which Meta acquired in 2019) and Rob Williams —
  raised $500M, with **Jeff Bezos nearly doubling an initial ~$50M commitment to ~$100M**, alongside
  Lux Capital and Google Ventures. The thesis is a bet *against* the current scaling playbook: instead
  of loosely "neural-network-inspired" software, Flourish uses **connectomics** (the detailed mapping
  of how neurons actually wire together in real brains) to design architectures targeting a **20–50
  watt** power budget — roughly what a biological brain runs on, and ~30× less than a single
  server-grade GPU. Its system is called Cortex AI. Why it matters: if model quality keeps scaling with
  energy, the industry hits a power wall; a credibly funded attempt to copy the brain's *efficiency*
  (not just its metaphor) is a real swing at that wall — though "reverse-engineer the brain's core
  algorithm" is a thesis that has defeated many well-funded teams before. `[single-source]` — [SiliconANGLE, 2026-06-04](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/04/ai-startup-flourish-reportedly-raises-500m-round-backed-jeff-bezos/) · [Crunchbase News, 2026-06-05](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/biggest-funding-rounds-june-5-2026/) `[via snippet]`

- **Supabase raises $500M at a $10.5B valuation — the "AI app backend" trade.** **Supabase**, the
  open-source **Postgres-based backend platform** (it gives developers a hosted database, auth, file
  storage, and auto-generated APIs so they don't build that plumbing themselves), closed $500M led by
  **GIC** at a $10.5B valuation. The AI angle is that the current wave of AI-assisted "vibe coding" and
  agent-generated apps still need a real database and backend underneath — Supabase is positioning as
  the default backend for code that AI writes. Why it matters: it's a clean read on where AI value is
  accruing — not only in the models, but in the unglamorous infrastructure the models' output runs on.
  — [Crunchbase News, 2026-06-05](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/biggest-funding-rounds-june-5-2026/) `[via snippet]`

- **AlphaSense raises $350M at a $7.5B valuation for AI market intelligence.** **AlphaSense**, which
  sells an AI-powered **market-intelligence** platform (it ingests filings, earnings transcripts,
  broker research, and news, then lets analysts search and summarize across all of it), raised $350M,
  lifting its valuation to $7.5B. It sits in the same "vertical AI for a specific knowledge-work
  profession" bucket — here, financial and corporate research — that keeps attracting nine-figure

## 🍎 Apple Silicon / on-device

Usually empty — today there's one concrete thread, riding the Nemotron release:

- **Nemotron 3 Ultra is already being quantized for Apple Silicon — within ~48h of the weights
  landing.** Community **MLX** ports (Apple's on-device ML framework) appeared on the Hub on June 5–6:
  6-bit, 6.2-bit, and 8-bit MLX builds (e.g. `pipenetwork/...MLX-8bit`,
  `inferencerlabs/...MLX-6.2bit`) plus a BF16 **GGUF** build (`AesSedai/...GGUF`) for the llama.cpp
  stack. Reality check on the headline number: even at 6-bit, a **550B** model is enormous —
  realistically a Mac Studio / 256GB-class unified-memory machine, not a laptop — so "runs on Apple
  Silicon" here means high-end desktop, not your MacBook Air. The genuinely laptop-friendly sibling is
  **Nemotron 3 Nano 4B**, which also got 4-bit and 8-bit MLX ports the same day and fits comfortably in
  consumer unified memory. Why it matters: the open-weights → quantized-local pipeline is now so fast
  that an Apple-Silicon-runnable build of a brand-new frontier-scale model exists within two days of
  release, no vendor involvement required. — [Nemotron 3 Ultra MLX 8-bit, Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/pipenetwork/NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Ultra-550B-A55B-MLX-8bit) · [Nemotron 3 Nano 4B MLX, Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/pipenetwork/NVIDIA-Nemotron-3-Nano-4B-MLX-8bit)
