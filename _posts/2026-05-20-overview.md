- **Microglia actively remodel fear-memory engrams during extinction learning.** Brain-resident immune cells are recruited to the soma and dendrites of fear-encoding neurons when mice undergo extinction training: somatic contact transiently silences the neurons, while dendritic contact triggers synapse pruning. Blocking microglial recruitment slowed extinction and preserved engram reactivity, providing a cellular-level mechanism for why exposure therapy reduces fear — with direct implications for PTSD.
