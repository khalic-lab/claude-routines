## 📄 ML research — second arXiv batch

- **arXiv:2605.24042** (2026-05-26) — *Hidden-State Privacy Has an Empty Middle* — Proves—theoretically via a Fisher-ball lower bound and empirically across 1,536 covariance configurations—that no single-layer Gaussian hidden-state release can simultaneously achieve moderate utility and moderate privacy against an adaptive Mahalanobis attacker. The diagonal inverse-Fisher mechanism is the unique minimax-optimal Gaussian release at first-order KL budget, but it sits on a knife-edge rather than an interior. Introduces a split-memory transformer that achieves 6–24× Mahalanobis-privacy advantage over standard GPT baselines at matched token budget; reframes the problem from mechanism design to architecture co-design. Strong implications for private inference deployments. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24042)

- **arXiv:2605.25114** (2026-05-26) — *Counterfactually Safe Reinforcement Learning* — Formalises individual harm from a counterfactual perspective: an action is harmful when it produces strictly worse outcomes than a designated baseline alternative. Proposes a two-stage policy-learning procedure that maximises expected return while bounding the individual harm rate, with finite-sample sub-optimality guarantees. Directly addresses a gap in safe RL: population-optimal policies may be individually harmful. Validated on both simulated and real-world datasets. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25114)

- **arXiv:2605.24749** (2026-05-26) — *How Neural Reward Models Learn Features for Policy Optimization: A Single-Index Analysis* — Studies feature learning in RLHF reward models under the key but under-analysed constraint that rewards are *exponentiated* to produce deployed policies. In a Gaussian single-index model, shows that exponential reward weighting alters the Hermite spectrum accessible to the first neural layer and derives tilted-policy value-gap bounds, yielding an explicit admissible temperature range that balances deployment gain against exponentially-amplified learning cost. Explains empirically observed sensitivity of RLHF pipelines to KL-penalty choices. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24749)

- **arXiv:2605.24055** (2026-05-26) — *Cascade-KDE: Robust Time-Series Restoration under Out-of-Distribution Impulse Corruptions* — Training-free restoration framework for industrial, healthcare, and energy time-series corrupted by large-magnitude impulse outliers. Estimates a 2D temporal-amplitude density, applies Density-Truncated Robust Expectation to bound outlier influence, then refines via exponential cascade with adaptive stopping. Preserves derivative peaks—critical for ECG morphology, battery degradation detection—better than classical filters and learning-based baselines. Relevant for anomaly-detection pipelines that use raw or lightly preprocessed sensor streams. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24055)

- **arXiv:2605.23978** (2026-05-26) — *Algometrics: Forecasting Under Algorithmic Feedback* — Introduces a framework for time series whose dynamics are shaped by the predictive algorithms deployed on them (algorithmic markets, RL environments). Proves three structural results: (1) deployment risk is *not identifiable* from passive historical data alone—infinitely many feedback environments share the same historical law but differ in deployment risk; (2) historical model rankings can *invert under crowding*, so lower passive error can mean higher deployment error; (3) randomised/instrumented actions identify short-horizon linear feedback with finite-sample bounds. Argues ML benchmarks in algorithmic settings must report feedback sensitivity alongside standard accuracy. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23978)
