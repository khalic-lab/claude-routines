**Anthropic ships Claude Opus 4.8 with GitHub Copilot GA and near-Mythos alignment rating** [vendor PR] [ongoing since 2026-05-28]

**Project Glasswing: Claude Mythos logs 23,019 vulnerabilities with 90.6% independent confirmation** [vendor PR] [ongoing since 2026-05-24]

Anthropic's May 26 update to Project Glasswing materially extends what was known five days ago. Claude Mythos Preview scanned more than 1,000 open-source projects and flagged 23,019 potential vulnerabilities; of those, an independent security review of a 1,752-finding sample confirmed 90.6% as real bugs — a validation rate that shifts the number from "AI-generated noise" to "actionable finding set." Anthropic estimates 6,202 issues are high- or critical-severity. The most striking individual finding: Mythos autonomously identified and fully exploited a 17-year-old remote code execution bug in FreeBSD (CVE-2026-4747) that grants complete server control. Downstream patching has started: Mozilla shipped 271 of the flagged bugs in Firefox 150 in a single release, and Cloudflare reports 2,000 findings across its critical infrastructure. The model remains in restricted access for Glasswing partners (AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorgan Chase, Linux Foundation, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Palo Alto Networks); general availability is contingent on Anthropic developing additional safeguards. Pricing for current partners is $25/$125 per million input/output tokens. — [Anthropic research blog, 2026-05-26](https://www.anthropic.com/research/glasswing-initial-update) [via snippet] · [Help Net Security, 2026-05-26](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/26/anthropic-project-glasswing-update/) [via snippet] · [The Hacker News, 2026-05-26](https://thehackernews.com/2026/05/claude-mythos-ai-finds-10000-high.html) [via snippet]

**OpenAI publishes Frontier Governance Framework mapping preparedness practices to EU AI Act and California law** [vendor PR]

OpenAI posted its Frontier Governance Framework on May 29, translating its existing internal Preparedness Framework into a public governance document oriented around two specific regulatory hooks: the EU AI Act's Code of Practice for General Purpose AI and California's Transparency in Frontier AI Act. The framework organises risk coverage into four threat categories — cyber offense, CBRN (chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear), harmful manipulation, and loss of control — and commits to structured processes for model reporting, security risk management, incident response, and external expert input. The document is explicit that the Preparedness Framework remains the internal foundation and that this external publication "applies relevant parts of that approach" rather than replacing it. Publishing now, well ahead of EU Code of Practice deadlines, is consistent with OpenAI's stated goal of positioning Frontier — the enterprise agent platform launched in February — as the compliance-ready option for regulated-industry buyers. — [OpenAI, 2026-05-29](https://openai.com/index/openai-frontier-governance-framework/) [via snippet] · [AI News, 2026-05-29](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/scaling-safe-enterprise-ai-openai-governance-frameworks/) [via snippet]

## 📊 Benchmarks & evaluations

**Glasswing: independent 90.6% true-positive rate as a methodology benchmark**

Project Glasswing's May 26 update is itself a data point on AI-assisted vulnerability research methodology. The independent firm validation of a 1,752-item sample is significant because AI security tooling has historically suffered from high false-positive rates that made findings operationally unusable. A 90.6% real-bug rate on a live open-source codebase — not a synthetic test set — is a meaningful bar. What remains unclear: the severity distribution of the confirmed bugs (the 90.6% figure applies to the full sample, not specifically to the 6,202 estimated high/critical) and whether the independent reviewers were blind to the AI's severity classification. The CVE-2026-4747 FreeBSD exploit finding, autonomously discovered and proven exploitable, is a qualitative demonstration that the tool can produce novel, non-trivial security research, though it also illustrates the dual-use tension that is driving Anthropic's restricted-access approach. [single-source for methodology detail] — [Anthropic research blog](https://www.anthropic.com/research/glasswing-initial-update) [via snippet]

**arXiv evaluation papers today**

Three evaluation frameworks were submitted to arXiv today that are worth tracking:

- **BenchTrace** [arXiv:2605.29225, cs.AI]: 1,821 annotated agent episodes testing LLM self-evolution; introduces "failure avoidance rate" as a new metric for measuring agent learning-from-failure. [preprint]
- **OpenClawBench** [arXiv:2605.29253, cs.AI]: 31,264 annotated agent execution trajectories focused on "process-side anomalies" — cases where an agent succeeds at the stated task while still exhibiting concerning intermediate behaviour. Addresses what the authors call the "Outcome-Process Gap." [preprint]
- **COLAGUARD** [arXiv:2605.29068, cs.AI]: a safety guardrail model that moves reasoning into latent space rather than generating explicit reasoning tokens, claiming a 12.9× speedup and 22.4× token reduction versus chain-of-thought-based safety classifiers. [preprint]

None of these have undergone peer review, and benchmark claims from preprints should be treated with caution until reproduced independently. [arXiv cs.AI, 2026-05-29](https://export.arxiv.org/rss/cs.AI)

## 💼 Industry, funding, regulation

**California's ~30 AI bills clear May 29 house-of-origin crossover deadline** [NEW]

Today is the California Legislature's house-of-origin deadline: the last day for a bill introduced in one chamber to pass that chamber or be shelved until next session. Nearly all of California's approximately 30 AI-related bills cleared the bar, meaning they advance to the second chamber on June 1. The legislative sprint that follows — from June 1 to the scheduled July 2 summer adjournment — is the critical window. Bills that survive it have a realistic path to Governor Newsom's desk before the end of the regular session. The specific bills in play cover a range of topics: automated decision-making transparency requirements, synthetic-media watermarking obligations, liability allocation for AI-generated harms, and AI use in hiring. California's position as the effective US AI regulator-of-last-resort (after the Colorado AI Act was gutted earlier this month and no federal framework has emerged) makes this pipeline consequential for the industry nationwide. Compliance teams should treat the June–July legislative outcome as the primary near-term regulatory signal. — [Transparency Coalition AI Legislative Update, 2026-05-29](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-may29-2026) [via snippet] · [Troutman Privacy Law Update, 2026-05-18](https://www.troutmanprivacy.com/2026/05/proposed-state-privacy-and-ai-law-update-may-18-2026/) [via snippet]
