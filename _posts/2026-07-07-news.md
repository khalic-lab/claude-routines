- <a id="st-4ed5174aeff4" class="st-a"></a>**Bern and the cantons weigh scrapping the mandatory three hours of weekly school sport.** Three hours of physical education a week are currently compulsory in Swiss schools. Under *Entflechtung 27* — a federal-cantonal project to re-sort who pays for and decides on various public tasks, now in its consultation phase — that obligation would be lifted, with the conference of cantonal education directors (EDK) telling SRF it sees no problem because cantonal curricula "adequately and conclusively" cover sport. Teachers and sport federations are pushing back hard: PE teacher Stefan Frei warns that dropping the guarantee makes sport a de facto elective and would worsen an already-visible rise in childhood inactivity, motor deficits and children who can't swim, especially in cash-strapped cantons tempted to trim. Aargau's education director backs devolving the choice to the cantons but says cutting sport hours has no political majority "when you see more and more overweight children." Associations, cities and communes can file positions until **Fri 10 July**; a first decision is expected in the autumn.
