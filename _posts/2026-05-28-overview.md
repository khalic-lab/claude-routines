- **Antihydrogen hyperfine splitting measured to 4 ppm — CPT symmetry test now within reach.** The ALPHA collaboration at CERN reports a measurement of the ground-state hyperfine splitting of antihydrogen at 4 parts per million precision. [_Nature_, 2026-05-27](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10556-x)

- **Direct dynamical black-hole mass in a 'little red dot' galaxy at high redshift.** Using gravitational lensing magnification, researchers made the first direct, dynamical measurement of a black hole mass in a high-redshift "little red dot" compact galaxy, confirming it hosts a massive overgrown black hole inconsistent with local scaling relations. Implies very early formation of supermassive black holes. [_Nature_, 2026-05-27](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10579-4)

- **Superallowed α-decay of ¹⁰⁴Te directly observed — fastest alpha emitter on record.** A nuclear physics experiment reports the first direct observation of the superallowed α-decay of ¹⁰⁴Te. Enhanced α-particle preformation in this neutron-deficient doubly-magic nucleus makes it the fastest known alpha emitter, providing a benchmark for nuclear shell models. [_Nature_, 2026-05-27](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10581-w)

- **Hippocampal CA3-to-CA1 sparse-to-dense coding transformation mapped.** A Nature study characterises how the hippocampus converts the sparse, pattern-separated representations in CA3 into denser CA1 codes, combining rapid learning capacity with efficient neural compression. The finding constrains computational models of hippocampal memory. [_Nature_, 2026-05-27](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10537-0)

- **Blood stem cells retain epigenetic memory of past inflammation.** Using xenograft inflammation–recovery models and single-cell multiomics, researchers identify a haematopoietic stem cell population that maintains durable epigenetic modifications after acute inflammatory stress, with implications for trained immunity and clonal haematopoiesis. [_Nature_, 2026-05-27](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10522-7)

- **Universal transcriptomic hallmarks of mammalian ageing identified across four species.** Integrating gene expression data from multiple tissues across four mammalian species, researchers find conserved transcriptomic signatures predicting both chronological age and mortality risk, pointing to shared molecular mechanisms of ageing across mammals. [_Nature_, 2026-05-27](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10542-3)
