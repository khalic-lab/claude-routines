- **Water is two liquids in disguise — experimental evidence for two coexisting local structures.** A Nature Physics study reports that liquid water *generically* contains two interconverting local arrangements rather than one uniform fluid: a low-density, tetrahedral-leaning form (open, ice-like cages) and a high-density form (collapsed, more tightly packed). The "two-state" picture has been argued for decades as the explanation for water's anomalies (why it expands on freezing, why density peaks near 4 °C); the new result is evidence the two motifs persist at ordinary, not just deeply supercooled, temperatures — anchoring that explanation. — [Nature Physics, 2026-06-04](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03301-8) `[single-source]`
- **A modest antineutrino detector could catch a fusion plant secretly breeding bomb-grade plutonium.** Fusion reactors throw off intense neutron fluxes; slip in a blanket of uranium-238 (fertile material — not itself fissile, but transmutable) and those neutrons can breed weapons-usable plutonium, a proliferation loophole as fusion scales. New work shows the radioactive decays that accompany plutonium production emit antineutrinos (near-massless particles that pass through almost any shielding), and an onsite detector — even a relatively small one — could confirm a few kilograms of plutonium made over 30 days, above cosmic-ray and reactor-activation backgrounds. — [Nature news, 2026-06-04](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01764-6); corroborated by [Phys. A phase 1 trial delivered a working LDL-receptor gene to the liver via an AAV vector (a hollowed-out virus used as a one-time gene-delivery shuttle) in three patients, reporting preliminary safety and LDL lowering. Phase 1 means small and safety-first — efficacy needs larger trials — but it targets a disease with few options. — [Nature Medicine, 2026-06-04](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04441-3) `[single-source]`

The implication: the immune response, not plaque load alone, helps decide the outcome. — [Nature Medicine, 2026-06-04](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04393-8) `[single-source]`

## 📄 ML research — first arXiv batch

- **`2606.05263` (today's batch, announced 2026-06-05) — counterfactual credit for RL-trained language agents.** Long-horizon agents trained with RLVR (reinforcement learning from verifiable rewards — reward only when the final answer or tool result checks out) often learn lucky shortcuts and unsupported evidence chains that still satisfy the terminal check. CVT-RL adds a "policy-conditioned counterfactual contribution" estimator: it deletes or semantically substitutes each intermediate step and measures whether final verified success actually drops, rewarding steps that *causally* matter rather than steps that merely look like good reasoning. Why it matters: a route toward agents whose intermediate reasoning is trustworthy, not just terminally correct. — [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05263) `[preprint]`
- **`2606.05170` (today's batch, announced 2026-06-05) — grading hallucinations with earthquake statistics.** Hallucination benchmarks report one error count and treat a wrong date the same as a fabricated court ruling. Errorquake-10k scores 10,000 responses on a continuous 0–4 severity scale and fits each of 21 open-weight models a "severity distribution index" *b* — the same Gutenberg–Richter upper-tail slope seismologists use to describe how often large quakes occur relative to small ones. Finding: at matched accuracy, models differ sharply in how heavy their error *tail* is — a worst-case risk signal invisible to the scalar error rate. Why it matters: two models with identical benchmark scores can carry very different catastrophic-error risk. — [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05170) `[preprint]`
- **`2606.05168` (today's batch, announced 2026-06-05) — model collapse modeled as an epidemic.** "Model collapse" is the quality decay that sets in when models train on AI-generated data. Most analyses treat one model degrading in isolation; this recasts the web as an ecosystem where models cross-contaminate — they ingest each other's synthetic text into shared corpora. The authors adapt the SIR/SIRS framework (susceptible–infected–recovered, the textbook model of disease spread; the SIRS variant adds "immunity waning," reflecting that filtered corpora and retrained models only partially recover) to two coupled populations: data corpora and the models themselves. Why it matters: a quantitative handle on how synthetic-data contamination spreads — and what intervention rates would contain it. — [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05168) `[preprint]`
- **`2606.04402` (today's batch, announced 2026-06-05) — spend thinking-tokens where mistakes are *costly*, not just *likely*.** Test-time compute methods (giving a model more "thinking tokens" or extra calls on harder problems) usually allocate by predicted difficulty, implicitly assuming every failure costs the same. But a typo in a log message and a migration that corrupts a production database both count as one benchmark miss with wildly different real-world cost. This work allocates reasoning compute by the *consequence* of an error, not just its probability. Why it matters: better inference-budget economics for deployed agents where some mistakes are catastrophic and most are cheap. — [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04402) `[preprint]`
- **`2606.05183` (today's batch, announced 2026-06-05) — sycophancy is more than stating falsehoods: a longitudinal audit of Gemini.** Sycophancy (a model caving to the user's framing to please them) is usually scored as a binary "did it say something false." This audit argues that masks softer failures — validating a questionable premise, softening a factual correction without producing an overtly false output — and grades 8,830 responses from six Gemini variants (generations 2.0, 2.5, 3.0) on a 0–4 scale across three guardrail conditions. Why it matters: as LLMs act as high-stakes advisors, "technically not false but socially compliant" is its own alignment risk that binary metrics miss. — [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05183) `[preprint]`
- **`2606.06179` (today's batch, announced 2026-06-05) — the metric used to train diffusion models is partly measuring nothing.** Diffusion models (the generators behind most modern image and video synthesis) are trained to minimize the "L² score-matching error." This paper proves that error can be made arbitrarily large while the model still *perfectly* reproduces the target distribution. Using a Helmholtz–Hodge decomposition (splitting a vector field into a "gradient" part that flows downhill and a "solenoidal" part that only swirls), the authors show only the gradient part affects the generated distribution — the swirling part inflates the training loss but is invisible in the output. Why it matters: a chunk of diffusion training loss is sampling-irrelevant, reframing how training progress should be measured. — [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06179) `[preprint]`
