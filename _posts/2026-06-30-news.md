## 🇨🇭 Switzerland & Vaud

- **Left and unions launch a referendum against parliament's curb on cantonal minimum wages.** The Socialists, Greens and the unions (Travail.Suisse, Unia) launched a referendum on Tuesday after the Federal Assembly voted to subordinate cantonal and communal minimum wages to extended collective bargaining agreements (CCTs — sector-wide pay deals between employers and unions) — meaning the CCT rate would prevail where one exists. PS co-president Cédric Wermuth called the reform "a bosses' coup d'état." Geneva and Neuchâtel, the two cantons whose minimum wages currently override CCTs, are most directly affected; Neuchâtel is separately weighing a cantonal referendum to defend cantonal sovereignty. ([Le Temps, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/une-attaque-un-coup-d-etat-des-patrons-la-gauche-lance-la-bataille-sur-le-salaire-minimum))
- **A broad alliance launches a referendum against building new nuclear plants.** Following parliament's decision to clear the legal path for new reactors, a wide coalition launched a referendum and must gather the 50,000 signatures by 8 October; a national vote would follow no earlier than February 2027. The fight reopens the post-Fukushima nuclear question — Switzerland had legislated a phase-out (no new plants) in 2017 — and pits new-build advocates against renewables-first critics who, like Valais MP Christophe Clivaz, call the nuclear revival "an ideological decision that slows renewables." ([SRF, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.srf.ch/news/schweiz/nach-beschluss-des-parlaments-breites-buendnis-lanciert-referendum-gegen-den-bau-neuer-akw); [Le Temps interview, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/christophe-clivaz-le-retour-du-nucleaire-est-une-decision-ideologique-qui-freine-les-renouvelables))
- **From 1 July, Switzerland may export less steel duty-free to the EU.** Bern and Brussels agreed new quotas under the EU's tightened steel safeguard regime taking effect 1 July: the bloc cuts total tariff-free import volumes by 47% to 18.3 million tonnes a year and raises the above-quota duty from 25% to 50%. Switzerland and eleven other partner states see their duty-free allowances fall ~33%. Brussels says it is responding to global overcapacity — China is named as the main culprit, with steel diverted to Europe after US trade barriers; the framework was negotiated under WTO rules in Geneva. ([Le Temps, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/des-le-1er-juillet-la-suisse-pourra-moins-exporter-d-acier-vers-l-ue))
- **E-ID rollout slips to 2027.** The introduction of Switzerland's state electronic identity (E-ID) is delayed, with the federal authorities citing the need to further harden data protection and security in light of new AI developments. The e-ID, narrowly approved by voters in 2025 after an earlier 2021 rejection, had been targeted for an earlier launch. ([SRF, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.srf.ch/news/schweiz/e-id-ab-2027-die-einfuehrung-der-e-id-verzoegert-sich)) `[single-source]`
- **SNB confirms it intervened to slow the franc's war-driven surge.** The Swiss National Bank sold francs to curb the currency's appreciation when the Iran war broke out, buying foreign exchange worth 3.9 billion francs (~$4.8bn) in the first quarter — the franc being a classic safe-haven that strengthens in crises, squeezing Swiss exporters. With its policy rate at zero, the SNB restated its readiness to intervene again "if necessary" even after the Middle East ceasefire. ([Le Temps, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/la-banque-nationale-est-intervenue-pour-freiner-la-hausse-du-franc-declenchee-par-la-guerre-au-moyen-orient); [Bloomberg, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/snb-intervened-to-halt-rush-for-franc-at-iran-war-outbreak))

## 🌍 World politics & geopolitics

- **US Supreme Court strikes down Trump's birthright-citizenship order, 6-3.** The court ruled unlawful the executive order Trump signed on his first day back in office that sought to deny citizenship to children born on US soil to non-citizen parents. Five justices held it violated the 14th Amendment (the post-Civil-War clause long read to grant citizenship to almost anyone born in the US); Justice Kavanaugh concurred on statutory rather than constitutional grounds; Thomas, Alito and Gorsuch dissented. Trump said he would now pursue the change through legislation. ([CNBC, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/30/supreme-court-birthright-citizenship-ruling.html); [PBS NewsHour, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/supreme-court-rejects-trump-limits-on-birthright-citizenship); [Le Temps, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/aux-etats-unis-la-cour-supreme-rejette-l-abrogation-du-droit-du-sol-par-donald-trump))
- **EU disburses its first €3.9bn for Ukrainian drones.** The European Commission paid out €3.9 billion earmarked for drone procurement — the first slice of a roughly €6bn tranche — drawn from the €90bn Ukraine Support Loan that backs Kyiv's budget and defence needs through 2026–27. The funds are meant to buy drones from Ukraine's own industry for its forces. ([European Commission, 30 Jun 2026](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_26_1490); [SRF, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.srf.ch/news/international/ukraine/krieg-in-der-ukraine-ukraine-erhaelt-von-der-eu-milliarden-fuer-drohnenbeschaffung))
- **Package bomb wounds a Ukrainian oligarch in Monaco; suspect at large.** A deliberate explosion late Monday (29 Jun) at a residential building near the French border wounded three people, including the apparent target, Dnipro real-estate magnate Vadym Yermolaiev, a Monaco resident under Ukrainian sanctions since December 2023. A couple in their 50s–60s suffered life-threatening injuries and a 13-year-old was hurt; prosecutors say the lone suspect, who left a package in the lobby, fled into France and there is so far no evidence of "terrorism." ([Al Jazeera, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/30/police-hunt-for-suspect-after-three-wounded-in-monaco); [France 24, 29 Jun 2026](https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20260629-monaco-residential-building-explosion-deliberate-attack))

Iran, for its part, says it will hold indirect talks through Qatar on implementing its memorandum of understanding (MoU — a non-binding framework agreement) with Washington, including frozen Iranian assets. ([SRF, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.srf.ch/news/international/nahost/krieg-im-nahen-osten-us-sondergesandte-in-katar-keine-gespraeche-mit-iran); [Al Jazeera, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/video/newsfeed/2026/6/30/iran-to-hold-talks-with-qatar-on-mou-deal-with-us-frozen-assets))

The measure was absent from the version deputies passed in early June; its return revives a controversy that drew more than two million signatures against the law last year. ([Le Temps, 30 Jun 2026](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/voterez-vous-pour-cette-horreur-en-adoptant-le-retour-de-ce-pesticide-les-senateurs-francais-reveillent-la-polemique-sur-la-loi-duplomb))
