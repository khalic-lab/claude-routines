## 🚀 New models, datasets, & open weights

- **MiniMax M3 — open-weight frontier-class coder with a 1M-token context and native
  multimodality.** Released to API on **June 1** (MiniMax Code, subscription plans, standard
  API); the open weights and technical report are promised on Hugging Face/GitHub **within
  ~10 days** — so today it is usable but not yet self-hostable, and no third party can
  inspect or reproduce it. The architecture's named novelty is **MSA (MiniMax Sparse
  Attention)** — sparse attention means the model only computes attention over a subset of
  past tokens rather than all of them, which is what makes a 1-million-token context (≈
  several books) tractable on cost and speed; MiniMax claims **15.6× faster decoding** and
  **9.7× faster prefill** versus its prior M2 at million-token contexts. M3 also takes image
  and video input and can drive a desktop ("computer use"). MiniMax bills it as the first
  open-weight model to combine frontier coding + 1M context + native multimodality in one.
  Why it matters: if the weights land and the claims hold, it is a self-hostable alternative
  to closed frontier coders at a fraction of the price. `[via snippet]` — [MiniMax official blog](https://www.minimax.io/blog/minimax-m3) `[via snippet]`; [VentureBeat, 2026-06-01](https://venturebeat.com/technology/minimax-m3-debuts-eclipsing-gpt-5-5-and-gemini-3-1-pro-on-key-benchmark-performance-for-just-5-10-of-the-cost) `[via snippet]`; [MarkTechPost, 2026-06-01](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/01/minimax-releases-minimax-m3-with-msa-architecture-supporting-1m-token-context-native-multimodality-and-agentic-coding/) `[via snippet]`; [South China Morning Post, 2026-06-01](https://www.scmp.com/tech/tech-trends/article/3355529/minimax-debuts-ai-model-built-long-and-complex-coding-tasks) `[via snippet]`. (Not yet on Hugging Face — a Hub search for "MiniMax M3" returned no repository as of this writing, consistent with the "weights within 10 days" promise.)

## 📊 Benchmarks & evaluations

- **MiniMax M3's "beats GPT-5.5 / Gemini 3.1 Pro" claims are company-reported and not yet
  independently verifiable.** The headline figure is **59.0% on SWE-Bench Pro** — a coding

  the weights aren't out, so no one outside MiniMax has reproduced them. A vendor SWE-Bench
