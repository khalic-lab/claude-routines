- **T. rex forelimb reduction tied to cranial enlargement, not body size** — UCL and Cambridge researchers analysed 82 theropod species and found arm shortening evolved independently in at least five lineages, in every case strongly correlated with skull and jaw enlargement rather than overall body mass. The study quantifies whether naturally occurring "white hydrogen" can scale to economical clean-energy production. Published in _PNAS_ (DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2603895123, 2026;123:21). Their 2026 programme targets reproof of Serre duality, GAGA, and Grothendieck–Hirzebruch–Riemann–Roch in complex-analytic geometry, providing a unified "analytic geometry" foundation. Quanta profiles both the mathematics and the researchers. [Quanta Magazine, 20 May 2026](https://www.quantamagazine.org/two-researchers-are-rebuilding-mathematics-from-the-ground-up-20260520/)
