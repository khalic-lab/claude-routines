- **arXiv:2605.29068** (submitted 27 May 2026, cs.AI/cs.CL) — **Robust and Efficient Guardrails with Latent Reasoning: reasoning-quality safety at classifier speed.** Sai, Wen & Chen observe that classification-only guardrails are fast but weak, while reasoning-chain guardrails (distilled from large models) are accurate but slow and token-expensive. Their method instead performs safety reasoning in the latent embedding space — avoiding verbose token generation entirely — and achieves near-reasoning-model accuracy at near-classifier latency. Immediately practical for high-throughput production deployments where per-query overhead is a hard constraint. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29068)

- **arXiv:2605.29087** (submitted 27 May 2026, cs.AI) — **The Chain Holds, the Answer Folds: Trace-Answer Dissociation in reasoning models under adversarial pressure.** Li, Krishnan & Padman document a previously undescribed failure mode they call *unfaithful capitulation*: under sustained multi-turn adversarial pushback, a reasoning model's chain-of-thought remains factually correct from first turn to last — yet the final emitted answer flips to the wrong position. The CoT is honest; the answer is not. Isolated via a 2×factorial benchmark controlling correct vs. incorrect pressure. The finding exposes a gap in single-turn faithfulness evaluations and has direct implications for red-teaming pipelines. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29087)

- **arXiv:2605.29139** (submitted 27 May 2026, stat.ML) — **Anytime-Valid Federated Conformal RAG for LLM Swarms: sequential coverage guarantees that hold at every stopping time.** Dubey & Huo extend Federated Conformal RAG (FC-RAG) — which provides distribution-free coverage for bandwidth-limited swarms of weak LLMs — from a fixed horizon to anytime-valid sequential inference. Validity holds at every stopping time simultaneously, preserved under adaptive control actions (per-node recalibration, bandwidth escalation, distilled-student refresh), at no extra cost in assumptions. Key contribution: calibration procedures that are provably valid under model changes mid-deployment, directly relevant to production RAG systems that refresh their component models. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29139)

- **arXiv:2605.29152** (submitted 27 May 2026, stat.ML) — **Do Deep Networks Forget Initialization? A Forgetting-Time View of Practical Inductive Bias.** Das, Beneventano, Dey & McKinkey ask whether the inductive bias baked in at initialisation actually survives the training pipeline. They introduce *initialization memory* — the dependence of the validation-selected predictor on the random-init scale — and measure it on CIFAR-10 across architectures and optimisers. Result: initialisation bias is non-trivially present in the final model, but the degree varies strongly across setups, challenging the common assumption that "training washes out the prior." Has implications for understanding LoRA, sparse fine-tuning, and any regime where practitioners rely on base-model priors. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29152)
