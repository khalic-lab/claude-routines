- **CVE-2026-42945 — CVSS 9.2 NGINX heap overflow under active exploitation** — A heap buffer overflow in ngx_http_rewrite_module affects NGINX versions 0.6.27 through 1.30.0 (an 18-year-old flaw). PoC was published 13 May; VulnCheck canaries flagged exploitation on 16 May. Unauthenticated DoS (worker-process crash) is reliable; RCE is currently conditional on ASLR being disabled. Patch exists and should be applied immediately by any organisation running NGINX with rewrite rules. [NVD CVE-2026-42945](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-42945) / [The Hacker News, May 2026](https://thehackernews.com/2026/05/nginx-cve-2026-42945-exploited-in-wild.html) / [bleepingcomputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/18-year-old-nginx-vulnerability-allows-dos-potential-rce/) [via snippet]

- **nginx-poolslip — unauthenticated RCE 0-day in NGINX 1.31.0, no CVE, no patch** — Researcher Vega of NebSec disclosed on 21 May a zero-day that bypasses ASLR to achieve full unauthenticated remote code execution on NGINX 1.31.0. The attack surface was not covered by the earlier nginx-rift patch. No CVE has been assigned; F5/NGINX has not released a fix. The full technical writeup is embargoed until a patch ships (estimated within 30 days). Operators running 1.31.0 should consider downgrading to 1.30.x (patched for CVE-2026-42945) pending a fix.
