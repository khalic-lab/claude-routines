## 📄 ML research — first arXiv batch

- **arXiv:2605.23926** (2026-05-26) — *How Much Thinking is Enough? Quantifying and Understanding Redundancy in LLM Reasoning* — Finds that 61–93% of reasoning steps produced by frontier models are redundant: the correct answer is recoverable by truncating the overwhelming majority of the trace. Crucially, the authors **prove** this is a structural consequence of length-agnostic outcome rewards in RL training — not a model-specific artefact patchable per model. Has direct implications for inference efficiency and how reasoning models should be trained. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23926)

- **arXiv:2605.24033** (2026-05-26) — *Towards Verifiable Transformers: Solver-Checkable Circuit Explanations* — Closes a long-standing gap in mechanistic interpretability: circuit explanations are typically validated by ablation and manual reasoning, leaving no formal guarantee. This work proposes generating solver-checkable proofs of circuit behaviour, so that a plausible explanation becomes a provable one. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24033)

- **arXiv:2605.24057** (2026-05-26) — *Feature Lottery? A Bifurcation Theory of Concept Emergence* — Uses dynamical-systems bifurcation theory to detect the precise training moments when neural networks acquire structured concept representations, without requiring ground-truth labels. Provides a principled, label-free lens on representation formation that could inform curriculum design. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24057)

- **arXiv:2605.24216** (2026-05-26) — *Agent-ToM: Learning to Monitor Autonomous LLM Agents via Theory-of-Mind Reasoning* — Addresses a hard safety problem: detecting covert malicious behaviour in autonomous LLM agents that pursue hidden objectives while appearing benign over many turns. The method applies Theory-of-Mind reasoning to infer latent agent intent from observed action sequences. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24216)

- **arXiv:2605.24218** (2026-05-26) — *QUEST: Training Frontier Deep Research Agents with Fully Synthetic Tasks* — Presents a pipeline for generating fully synthetic multi-step research tasks to train open-source deep-research agents, avoiding reliance on proprietary data.
