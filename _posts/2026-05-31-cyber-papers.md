## 🇨🇭 Switzerland & Vaud

- **Financing the army through a VAT hike is not winning the room.** As Bern looks for ways to fund the planned ramp-up in defence spending, the option of raising the *TVA/Mehrwertsteuer* (value-added tax — the consumption tax everyone pays at the till) is meeting open skepticism across the political spectrum. The objection is distributional: a VAT increase is regressive (it takes a larger share of income from poorer households than richer ones), so paying for tanks and air defence at the supermarket checkout is a hard sell. The debate matters because Switzerland has committed to lifting military outlays toward 1% of GDP, and every financing route — VAT, debt-brake exceptions, or cuts elsewhere — has a constituency against it. [Le Temps, 2026-05-31](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/augmenter-le-budget-de-l-armee-par-une-hausse-de-la-tva-ne-convainc-pas) `[single-source]`
- **Excess deaths at a Zurich hospital revive the case for systematic hospital comparison.** After higher-than-expected mortality (more patient deaths than the case mix would predict) was flagged at a Zurich facility, the policy conversation is shifting toward routinely benchmarking outcomes across Swiss hospitals. The underlying tension is that Switzerland has world-class healthcare but historically thin public outcome transparency — comparing raw death rates is misleading without *case-mix adjustment* (correcting for how sick each hospital's patients are to begin with), which is exactly the methodological work being called for. [Le Temps, 2026-05-31](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/apres-la-surmortalite-detectee-a-zurich-vers-davantage-de-comparaisons-entre-les-hopitaux-suisses) `[single-source]`
- **A quiet backlash is building against data centres.** Le Temps reports mounting local resistance to the data-centre boom in Switzerland — the warehouse-scale server farms behind cloud and AI workloads. The friction points are the familiar ones now surfacing across Europe: electricity draw (a single large facility can consume as much power as a small town), water use for cooling, and land. For a country that markets itself as a stable, low-latency hosting hub with cheap hydro and strong data-protection law, the political question is whether that growth model survives local opposition and grid constraints. [Le Temps, 2026-05-31](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/une-colere-sourde-monte-contre-les-centres-de-donnees) `[single-source]`
- **Theatre as a fraud vaccine: training seniors against "shock calls."** Swiss public broadcaster SRF profiles a prevention scheme that uses live theatre to inoculate older residents against *Schockanrufe* — "shock calls," the scam where a caller impersonates a relative or police officer ("your daughter had an accident, we need bail money now") to panic the victim into handing over cash or valuables. It is a social-engineering attack that no software patch fixes; the defence is recognising the script, and rehearsing the emotional ambush on a stage is a concrete way to build that reflex. A useful reminder that the Swiss cyber-fraud surface includes the telephone, not just the inbox. [SRF, 2026-05-31](https://www.srf.ch/news/schweiz/neue-praevention-gegen-betrueger-theater-gegen-schockanrufe-ihre-tochter-hatte-einen-unfall) `[single-source]`

## 🛡️ Cybersecurity

- **Nx Console supply-chain compromise escalates: ~3,800 GitHub internal repositories exfiltrated.** _Escalation of yesterday's story._ The malicious build of the **Nx Console** VS Code extension (**CVE-2026-48027**, CVSS 9.3 — critical) did far more damage than the narrow 18-minute marketplace exposure window first suggested. CISA's 28 May alert and follow-on reporting put the blast radius at roughly **3,800 of GitHub's own internal repositories** stolen, with OpenAI, Mistral AI, and Grafana Labs also named as impacted. The mechanics are the textbook nightmare of a *supply-chain compromise* (poisoning a trusted upstream dependency so victims install malware via normal updates): VS Code's auto-update silently pushed the trojanised 18.95.0 to machines that already had the extension, and its payload swept up GitHub/npm tokens, AWS and GCP credentials, SSH private keys, Vault and 1Password CLI sessions, and Kubernetes auth. The compromise is linked to a parallel poisoning of **TanStack** npm packages (**CVE-2026-45321**) and a malicious **Daemon Tools Lite** build (**CVE-2026-8398**) — all three added to CISA's KEV catalog on 27 May. Remediation: upgrade Nx Console to **18.100.0** and rotate every credential a developer machine could have touched. Why it matters: this is a developer-credential heist that turns one stolen maintainer login into thousands of downstream private-repo breaches. [CISA alert, 2026-05-28](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/05/28/supply-chain-compromises-impact-nx-console-and-github-repositories); [The Hacker News, 2026-05-29](https://thehackernews.com/2026/05/github-internal-repositories-breached.html); [NVD CVE-2026-48027](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-48027). `[ongoing since 2026-05-30]`
- **OpenCATS: two authenticated SQL-injection flaws in the DataGrid.** Today's NVD batch published a pair of *SQL injection* vulnerabilities (where attacker-controlled input is smuggled into a database query, letting it read or alter data it shouldn't) in **OpenCATS**, a widely used open-source applicant-tracking/recruiting system. **CVE-2026-49489** (CVSS 8.6, high) lives in the `sortDirection` parameter of `ajax/getDataGridPager.php` and supports *time-based blind* extraction — the attacker reads the database one yes/no question at a time by measuring how long the server takes to respond. **CVE-2026-49490** (CVSS 8.4, high) abuses crafted filters to reach the supposedly non-filterable Tags column in the Candidates DataGrid, bypassing the app's own column restrictions. Both require an authenticated account but then allow extraction of database contents — meaning candidate PII and recruiter data. Public exploit code already exists (Exploit-DB, Packet Storm). Patch via the vendor advisories. [NVD CVE-2026-49489](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-49489); [NVD CVE-2026-49490](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-49490); [OpenCATS advisory GHSA-8mc8-5gw6-c7w4](https://github.com/opencats/OpenCATS/security/advisories/GHSA-8mc8-5gw6-c7w4).

Individually low-newsworthiness and mostly affecting end-of-life hardware, but collectively they are exactly the population that gets conscripted into IoT botnets — the practical takeaway is that unpatched, internet-exposed consumer routers remain the cheapest mass-compromise target on the internet.
