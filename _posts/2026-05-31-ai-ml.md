## 🍎 Apple Silicon / on-device

This is where the day actually had a pulse — two strands, both local-inference
tooling.

**`llama.cpp` kept up its weekend build cadence (b9434 → b9442, 2026-05-30 to
-31).** `llama.cpp` is the C/C++ engine most people use to run LLMs locally on a
Mac, PC, or phone without a cloud. It ships dozens of tagged builds a week; what's
genuinely *new* since this brief last touched it (b9371, 2026-05-28, which was
about WebGPU) are these concrete changes:

- **Qwen 3.5/3.6 tensor-parallelism granularity fix on 3-GPU setups** (b9434).
  Tensor parallelism splits one model's layers across multiple GPUs so a model too
  big for one card still runs; the fix corrects how work is partitioned when the
  GPU count (3) doesn't divide evenly — matters for anyone running the larger Qwen
  open-weights across a mixed multi-GPU rig.
- **`-fa auto` added to `llama-bench`** (b9437). `-fa` is the flash-attention
  toggle (a memory-efficient attention kernel that speeds up long contexts);
  `auto` lets the benchmark tool pick it per backend instead of forcing it on/off.
- **`-ngl` now defaults to -1** (b9437) — `-ngl` ("number of GPU layers") controls
  how many model layers are offloaded to the GPU; `-1` means "all of them," a
  saner default than the old fixed number for users who just want full GPU use.
- **Single iGPU device by default** (b9439) — on machines with an integrated GPU
  it now picks one rather than fanning out, avoiding a class of misconfiguration.
- **OpenCL backend now handles bf16 by down-converting to f16** (b9436) — lets
  bf16 ("brain-float-16", a 16-bit number format common in modern checkpoints)
  models load on OpenCL hardware that only speaks f16.
- Plus a `jina-embeddings-v2-base-zh` tokenizer addition (b9442) and themeable
  prebuilt binaries via custom-CSS injection (b9438).

Why it matters: none of these is headline-grade alone, but together they're the
steady maintenance that keeps recent Chinese open-weights (Qwen, GLM) and embedding
models runnable on consumer and Apple hardware. [[llama.cpp releases, b9434–b9442](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/releases/tag/b9442)] _[ongoing since 2026-05-28]_

**A wave of MLX 4-bit community ports hit Hugging Face today (2026-05-30 to -31).**
MLX is Apple's array/ML framework for running models natively on Apple-Silicon
(M-series) Macs; "4-bit" means the weights are quantized down to 4 bits each, which
roughly quarters memory use versus 16-bit so a model fits on a laptop, at a small
accuracy cost. These are *community repackagings of existing models*, not new
weights — but they're exactly what makes those models usable on a MacBook:

- **`mlx-community/SongGeneration-v2` (medium + large), `SoulX-Singer`** — 4-bit
  Mac ports of audio-generation models (song generation, singing-voice synthesis),
  posted 2026-05-30/-31. [[SongGeneration-v2-large-4bit](https://huggingface.co/mlx-community/SongGeneration-v2-large-4bit)]
- **`mlx-community/Lens-3.8B-4bit`** — Mac port of Microsoft's Lens text-to-image
  diffusion model (2026-05-31). [[card](https://huggingface.co/mlx-community/Lens-3.8B-4bit)]
- **`clicshum/Qwen3.6-27B-MLX-4bit`** — a 4-bit MLX port of the 27.8B Qwen VLM,
  bringing a mid-size open-weight vision-language model onto Apple Silicon
  (2026-05-31). [[card](https://huggingface.co/clicshum/Qwen3.6-27B-MLX-4bit)]
- **`SirSahOl/glm-edge-{1.5b,4b}-chat-mlx-4bit`** — Mac ports of Z.ai's small
  GLM-Edge chat models, the kind sized for phones/laptops (2026-05-30). [[glm-edge-4b](https://huggingface.co/SirSahOl/glm-edge-4b-chat-mlx-4bit)]
- **`beshkenadze/moondream3-preview-mlx-4bit`** — Mac port of the 9.3B moondream3
  compact VLM (2026-05-30). [[card](https://huggingface.co/beshkenadze/moondream3-preview-mlx-4bit)]

These are low-download fresh uploads (community, not vendor), so weight them as
ecosystem texture, not a release event. The signal is that the open-weights → Apple
Silicon conversion pipeline is fast and broad right now, spanning text, vision, and
audio models within a day or two of interest. The Weekend brief covers on-device
depth; this is the daily glance.
