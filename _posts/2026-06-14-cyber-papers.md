## 🇨🇭 Switzerland & Vaud

Today was an **Abstimmungssonntag** — a federal voting Sunday, the recurring fixed date on which Switzerland's direct democracy decides national ballot questions and many cantons run their own votes and elections in parallel. Two federal items were on the ballot, and the results reshape the immigration and Europe debate for the rest of the year.

- **Switzerland rejects the SVP's "No 10-million Switzerland" initiative, 54.8% No on 58.9% turnout — the headline result of the day.** `[ongoing since 2026-05-23]` The initiative, from the right-wing Swiss People's Party (SVP), would have written into the federal constitution a hard cap keeping the permanent resident population below ten million people until 2050, forcing the government to throttle immigration (and, critics warned, reopen the free-movement deal with the EU) if the number approached the ceiling. Voters said no by 54.8% to 45.2%, with turnout an unusually high 58.9% — well above the ~45% typical for federal ballots, a sign the migration question mobilised the electorate. The margin is decisive but not a landslide: as SRF notes, past SVP migration initiatives have been knife-edge (the 2014 "mass immigration" initiative *passed* with 50.3%), so 54.8% No reads as the country endorsing the *diagnosis* — that growth strains housing and infrastructure — while rejecting the SVP's blunt *remedy* of a fixed cap. Why it matters: a Yes would have put Bern on a collision course with Brussels over free movement; the No keeps that door shut. ([SRF, 2026-06-14](https://www.srf.ch/news/schweiz/abstimmung-vom-14-juni-initiative-keine-10-mio-schweiz-gescheitert); [Le Temps live results, 2026-06-14](https://www.letemps.ch/suisse/en-direct-les-votations-du-14-juin-resultats-et-analyses))

- **The narrowest result of the day: voters approve the civil-service (Zivildienst) reform by roughly 52.5% Yes.** `[ongoing since 2026-06-07]` Switzerland keeps conscription for men; the civilian service (Zivildienst) is the alternative for conscientious objectors who don't want to serve in the army. The reform — backed narrowly, about 52.5% Yes (a "oui de la méfiance," a yes born of distrust of the army, as Le Temps frames it) — tightens that off-ramp: it makes switching from the military to civilian service harder, to stem an outflow the army says is draining its ranks. SRF's read is that voters approved the tightening but explicitly **not** as a "free pass" for further hardening down the line — a cautious yes. Why it matters: it's a quiet but real recalibration of Switzerland's militia-army staffing at a time when European defence postures are tightening. ([Le Temps, 2026-06-14](https://www.letemps.ch/articles/le-peuple-siffle-la-fin-de-l-echappatoire-du-service-civil); reform passage figure [Le Temps live results, 2026-06-14](https://www.letemps.ch/suisse/en-direct-les-votations-du-14-juin-resultats-et-analyses) [via snippet]; reaction context [SRF, 2026-06-14](https://www.srf.ch/news/schweiz/zivildienstgesetz-ja-zur-reform-aber-kein-freipass-fuer-weitere-verschaerfungen))

## 🛡️ Cybersecurity

A quieter day for net-new disclosures — much of this week's heavy traffic (the Ivanti Sentry CVSS-10 root RCE, the Oracle PeopleSoft and Check Point KEV additions, the Cisco/Arista/Chromium KEV batch) was already covered in the 09–13 June briefs and is dropped here as already-reported. Today's National Vulnerability Database (NVD) window surfaced six new CVEs; three are worth flagging.

- **CVE-2026-54420 — LiteSpeed cPanel plugin symlink flaw, CVSS 8.5 (HIGH), exploited in the wild.** The LiteSpeed cPanel plugin (a web-server add-on for the cPanel/WHM hosting control panel) mishandles symbolic links — symlinks, filesystem pointers that redirect one path to another — supplied by a user who already has FTP or web-shell access on a *shared* hosting server running CloudLinux/CageFS. CageFS is the isolation layer meant to box each customer into their own jailed view of the filesystem; the bug lets a tenant use a crafted symlink to escape that box and reach files outside their jail. NVD records active exploitation in the wild in May 2026. Note this is a **distinct** vulnerability from the LiteSpeed cPanel privilege-escalation flaw (CVE-2026-48172) reported in May — same product line, different bug, so treat it as a fresh patch obligation, not a rerun. Fix: LiteSpeed cPanel plugin 2.4.8 / WHM plugin 5.3.2.0 or later. Why it matters: shared-hosting tenant isolation is exactly the boundary providers sell as safe; a working in-the-wild escape puts every co-tenant on an unpatched box at risk. ([NVD, 2026-06-14](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-54420))

- **CVE-2026-11526 & CVE-2026-11527 — OS command injection in two widely-used Perl CPAN modules via the classic 2-argument `open()` trap.** Two popular Perl libraries — `GD` (the image-manipulation module, before v2.86) and `Config::IniFiles` (an INI-config parser, before v3.001000) — open a caller-supplied filename using Perl's **two-argument `open()`**. This is a decades-old Perl footgun: with two-arg `open()`, a filename that begins or ends with a pipe (`"| cmd"`, `"cmd |"`) or starts with a redirect (`"> path"`) is *executed as a shell command* rather than opened as a file. So any application that passes attacker-influenced filenames into these modules can be coerced into running arbitrary OS commands or overwriting files. Why it matters: these are foundational, transitively-depended-on libraries — the impact isn't one app but the long tail of Perl software (still heavy in sysadmin tooling, monitoring, and legacy web stacks) that feeds user input into them. Upgrade `GD` to ≥2.86 and `Config::IniFiles` to ≥3.001000. ([NVD CVE-2026-11526, 2026-06-14](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-11526); [NVD CVE-2026-11527, 2026-06-14](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-11527))

- **CVE-2026-54421 — OpenStack Ironic leaks iSCSI credentials on a volume PATCH, CVSS 6.8 (MEDIUM).** OpenStack Ironic is the bare-metal provisioning service in OpenStack clouds (it manages physical servers the way Nova manages VMs). When a user issues a PATCH request (an HTTP partial-update) to modify volume-property fields they're authorised to change, Ironic (through v35.0.1) can return *unredacted* sensitive information in the response — including iSCSI credentials (the username/password used to attach networked block storage). The data is only exposed on the PATCH (update) path, not on POST (create). Why it matters: leaked storage credentials can let a tenant reach block storage they shouldn't, a lateral-movement foothold inside a private/hybrid cloud. ([NVD, 2026-06-14](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-54421))
