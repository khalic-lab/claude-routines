- **arXiv:2605.26343** (2026-05-27) — *MechRL: Reinforcement Learning Agents Perform Circuit Discovery for Mechanistic Interpretability* — Recasts the labour-intensive circuit-discovery problem in mechanistic interpretability as an RL task: an agent operates over the 144 attention heads of GPT-2 small as a discrete action space, learning to identify the minimal attention-head subsets (circuits) that implement specific model behaviours. The approach bypasses the bespoke analytical pipelines currently needed for each new task, and opens the door to scalable automated interpretability. A strong cross-disciplinary contribution at the RL × interpretability intersection. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26343)

- **arXiv:2605.26526** (2026-05-27) — *Open-Weight LLM Fine-Tuning Defenses are Susceptible to Simple Attacks* — Shows that existing defenses designed to prevent adversarial misuse of open-weight LLMs via fine-tuning share a flawed premise: they assume harmful behaviour must be *learned* through fine-tuning, whereas pretrained LLMs already encode substantial harmful knowledge that can be *elicited* without new training. Simple attack strategies that exploit this latent knowledge bypass current defenses entirely, challenging the viability of post-hoc safety layers on open-weight releases. Directly relevant to enterprise red-teaming and open-source model governance. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26526)

- **arXiv:2605.26282** (2026-05-27) — *Scaling World-Model Reinforcement Learning Through Diffusion Policy Optimization* — Identifies a structural misalignment between the policy gradient objective and world-model-generated rollouts as the principal (and underappreciated) bottleneck limiting scale in MBRL beyond model bias. Addresses this via diffusion-based policy optimisation that jointly models the distribution of world-model trajectories and policy updates, achieving improved performance on long-horizon control tasks and demonstrating that MBRL can scale more reliably with model capacity when the misalignment is corrected. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26282)

- **arXiv:2605.26496** (2026-05-27) — *Dense2MoE: Pushing the Pareto Frontier of On-Device LLMs via Unified Pruning and Upcycling* — Existing approaches to converting dense LLMs into Mixture-of-Experts architectures for on-device deployment either upcycle (retaining parameter redundancy) or prune (destroying expert diversity). Dense2MoE jointly prunes and upcycles in a unified objective, eliminating redundant parameters while preserving routing diversity, and achieves a better trade-off between model size, inference latency, and accuracy than either approach alone. Particularly relevant for edge/mobile deployment of large reasoning models. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26496)

- **arXiv:2605.26289** (2026-05-27) — *Stateful Inference for Low-Latency Multi-Agent Tool Calling* — Observes that 85–95% of the prompt is unchanged between consecutive tool-call turns in multi-agent LLM workflows, yet existing inference frameworks re-process the full conversation from scratch on every turn at O(n_t) cost. Proposes a stateful inference architecture that caches and reuses prior-turn KV state, converting per-turn complexity to O(delta_t), and reports latency reductions that make sub-second round trips feasible at production scale. [preprint] [Abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26289)
