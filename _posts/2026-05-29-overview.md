## 🔬 Science

- **Experimental randomness amplification realised with superconducting qubits.** Kulikov, Storz, Wallraff, Renner et al. (ETH Zurich) close the loophole on a foundational quantum-information task: using a Bell test on a 2-qubit superconducting circuit, they amplify a weakly random seed into certified, device-independent randomness — a result required for the strongest quantum cryptographic proofs. — [_Nature_, 27 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10521-8)

- **β-Arrestin proteins form liquid-liquid phase-separated condensates that diversify GPCR signalling.** Anderson, Xiao, Rajagopal et al. show that β-arrestins — the multifunctional scaffold proteins that desensitise G-protein-coupled receptors — undergo LLPS under physiological conditions, creating spatial hubs that partition receptor trafficking from signalling. The condensates appear across multiple GPCR subtypes, suggesting a general regulatory mechanism with broad pharmacological implications. — [_Nature_, 27 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10539-y)

- **Darkness and body size drove selectivity of end-Cretaceous marine extinction.** Ying, Monteiro, Witts & Schmidt (Bristol) couple an impact-winter climate model to a global marine-fossil database. Light deprivation from the ejecta dust veil emerges as the dominant kill mechanism, selectively eliminating large-bodied, light-dependent plankton and zooplankton while small, generalist scavengers survived — resolving a long-standing debate about why extinction severity was so size-dependent. — [_Nature_, 27 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10541-4)

- **Cavity photons induce measurable attractive interactions in a semiconductor quantum material.** Helmrich, Adlong, Kroner, İmamoğlu et al. (ETH Zurich) place exciton polaritons in a THz cavity and observe cavity-mediated attractive exciton–exciton interactions, a long-sought ingredient for cavity-enhanced quantum phase transitions. The effect is tunable by cavity detuning, pointing toward photon-driven correlated phases in 2D materials. — [_Nature_, 27 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10609-1)

- **Gene therapies for heart failure regain momentum after years of setbacks.** Nature news reviews a new wave of cardiac gene therapy trials — SERCA2a upregulation (rescuing calcium cycling), small inhibitory RNA against phospholamban, and myosin activators — now entering Phase 2/3 after early trials stalled on inadequate dosing and delivery vectors. The piece covers how AAV capsid engineering and intracoronary delivery improvements are restarting the field. — [_Nature_ news, 28 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01598-2)

## 📄 ML research — first arXiv batch

- **arXiv:2605.28860** (29 May 2026) — **RL fine-tuning preserves transformer circuits better than SFT — mechanistic analysis.** Rojas Nunez, Sawant, Allen et al. introduce *differential circuit vulnerability*, a head-level metric measuring how fine-tuning degrades computational circuits. Comparing RL and SFT on Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct adapted to scientific QA, they find RL retains a larger fraction of base-model attention circuits at the cost of slower task convergence, offering a mechanistic explanation for why RL generalises more robustly. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28860)

- **arXiv:2605.28839** (29 May 2026) — **One mask reveals the shared mechanism behind knowledge edits in ROME and MEMIT.** Holmov, Youssef, Schoots & Seifert train a compact binary mask over edited MLP weights and find it reverses ~80% of ROME/MEMIT edits on a training set and >70% on held-out facts. The mask targets a common functional subspace regardless of which fact was edited, and works by eliminating over-attention in later layers. Key implication: edits *suppress* rather than overwrite stored knowledge, explaining why factual changes fail to propagate to related queries. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28839)

- **arXiv:2605.28889** (29 May 2026) — **Context distillation as latent memory management: LoRA adapter banks with self-gating.** Zheng, Li, Wen et al. (CUHK / Huawei) distil each context into an independent LoRA adapter forming a modular memory bank. A Self-Gating mechanism decides at query time whether to activate the retrieved adapter, improving robustness when no stored memory is relevant. Cache sharing reduces inference overhead; the framework substantially outperforms RAG-with-retrieval baselines. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28889)

- **arXiv:2605.29198** (29 May 2026, cs.CV/cs.LG) — **GCPO: per-token credit assignment via guidance-contrastive policy optimisation.** Zhang et al. contrastively compare model predictions under positive and negative prompts to assign token-level advantages, replacing the uniform sample-level credit of GRPO/DAPO. Applied to text-to-image generation and chain-of-thought reasoning, GCPO visually concentrates gradient signal on semantically relevant regions and consistently outperforms GRPO/DAPO across both task types. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29198)

- **arXiv:2605.28862** (29 May 2026) — **TRACE: trajectory-aware LLM agent for molecular lead optimisation.** Li, Zhang, Fan et al. frame ADMET lead optimisation as sequential tool selection over action trajectories rather than single-step edits, enabling forward-looking structural refinement while preserving binding-affinity substructures. Outperforms baselines on multiple ADMET tasks on validity, property improvement, and molecular similarity retention. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28862)

- **arXiv:2605.28870** (29 May 2026) — **Platonic Representation Hypothesis grounded in linear structure and word-frequency noise.** Bangachev, Bresler & Polyanskiy (MIT) propose a tripartite signal/bias/noise decomposition: cross-modal alignment arises from a universal linear object–attribute encoding (Linear Representation Hypothesis), while representational noise is driven by data scarcity — confirmed by a strong positive correlation between word frequency and cross-model alignment in LLMs. Centering and normalisation partially corrects for model-specific bias. [preprint] — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28870)
