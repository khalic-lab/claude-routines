## 🚀 New models, datasets, & open weights

**1. DeepSeek V4 Pro** | DeepSeek AI | April 24, 2026

- **Parameters:** 1.6T total / 49B active (MoE); companion V4 Flash at 284B total / 13B active
- **Context:** 1M tokens (default across all DeepSeek services)
- **License:** MIT
- **Weights:** [huggingface.co/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro](https://huggingface.co/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro)
- **Architecture:** Hybrid Compressed Sparse Attention + Heavily Compressed Attention for long-context efficiency; Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections replace conventional residual links.
- **Training hardware:** Huawei Ascend 950 chips + Cambricon accelerators — zero Nvidia hardware; Huawei "Supernode" interconnect fabric.
- **Benchmarks (vendor-claimed):** SWE-bench Verified 80.6%; Codeforces rating 3,206 (highest reported at release, ahead of GPT-5.4 at 3,168).
- **Pricing:** $0.145/M input, $1.74/M output — approximately 7× cheaper than GPT-5.5 at comparable capability tiers.
- **NIST/CAISI independent evaluation** (May 2026): Finds V4 Pro "lags frontier by approximately 8 months," performing comparably to GPT-5 on non-public benchmarks. Most cost-efficient PRC model evaluated to date. (See Benchmarks section.)
- [[vendor PR] DeepSeek API docs, April 24 2026](https://api-docs.deepseek.com/news/news260424); [NIST CAISI, May 2026](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/05/caisi-evaluation-deepseek-v4-pro)

**2. Microsoft and OpenAI restructure partnership — Azure exclusivity ends** _(April 27)_

Both companies jointly announced amendments to their 2019 agreement. Key changes:
- OpenAI can now distribute all products via AWS, Google Cloud, and other providers — Azure exclusivity is over.
- Microsoft remains OpenAI's "primary cloud partner"; OpenAI ships first on Azure unless Microsoft cannot support the required capability.
- Microsoft ends its revenue-share payments _to_ OpenAI. OpenAI continues paying Microsoft a capped revenue share through 2030, independent of technology milestones.
- Microsoft retains ~27% equity stake (valued ~$135B at the October 2025 restructuring).
- IP license (non-exclusive) runs to 2032.
- The AGI clause — which gave Microsoft the right to assess whether OpenAI had reached AGI — has been simplified and removed.
- T1: [Microsoft Blog, April 27 2026](https://blogs.microsoft.com/blog/2026/04/27/the-next-phase-of-the-microsoft-openai-partnership/); [OpenAI, April 27 2026](https://openai.com/index/next-phase-of-microsoft-partnership/); T2: [CNBC, April 27 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/27/openai-microsoft-partnership-revenue-cap.html)

**3. EU AI Act: Digital Omnibus trilogue fails; August 2 deadline intact** _(April 28; ongoing)_

The proposed Digital Omnibus would delay high-risk AI compliance deadlines from **August 2, 2026** to December 2, 2027 (stand-alone Annex III systems) and August 2, 2028 (AI embedded in regulated products). The **April 28 trilogue** between Parliament, Council, and Commission ended without agreement — the second failure. The **next trilogue is scheduled for May 13, 2026**. If no deal is adopted before August 2, the current Act applies with no extensions.

As of April 30, no delay is legally in effect. Organisations deploying high-risk AI in the EU should maintain compliance posture against the August 2 date, not the proposed extended deadline.
- T2: [IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/eu-ai-act-reform-talks-stall-as-key-compliance-deadline-looms); [Holland & Knight, April 2026](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/us-companies-face-eu-ai-acts-possible-august-2026-compliance-deadline); [DLA Piper GENIE](https://knowledge.dlapiper.com/dlapiperknowledge/globalemploymentlatestdevelopments/2026/The-Digital-AI-Omnibus-Proposed-deferral-of-high-risk-AI-obligations-under-the-AI-Act)
