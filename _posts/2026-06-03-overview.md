## 🔬 Science

- **Hong–Ou–Mandel quantum interference pushed past ten atoms at once.** HOM interference (the effect where two genuinely identical particles hitting a beamsplitter always exit the same side together, never splitting — a direct, all-or-nothing signature of quantum indistinguishability) has long been a two-photon party trick. A new experiment extends it to more than ten indistinguishable atoms simultaneously, a step toward engineered many-body quantum interference and atom-based quantum technologies. Why it matters: controlling indistinguishability at this scale is a prerequisite for atom-interferometry sensors and bosonic quantum simulation. [Nature Physics, 2026-06-01](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03302-7) `[single-source]`
- **Thermal noise of spin waves "squeezed" for the first time in single- and two-mode form.** Magnons are quantized spin waves — the collective wobble of electron spins in a magnet. "Squeezing" redistributes noise so that one measurable quantity dips below the usual quantum/thermal floor at the expense of its partner. Demonstrating this for thermal magnon modes opens a route to lower-noise magnonic sensors and on-chip quantum information carried by spin rather than charge. [Nature Physics, 2026-06-01](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03294-4) `[single-source]`
- **Embryos solidify their tissues via stickiness, not crowding.** Tissues can behave like a fluid or jam into a solid — "jamming" being the same physics that makes packed grains or coffee beans suddenly lock up. This study shows that in embryonic epithelia the fluid-to-solid (rigidity) transition is driven by cell–cell adhesion, decoupled from the density-driven jamming the field often conflated it with. Why it matters: it clarifies the physical knob embryos actually turn to sculpt themselves into shape. [Nature Physics, 2026-06-02](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03276-6) `[single-source]`
- **Defined 15-strain bacterial "pill" goes head-to-head with fecal transplant for recurrent C. difficile.** Recurrent _Clostridioides difficile_ infection — a gut infection that keeps relapsing after antibiotics wipe out competing microbes — is often beaten back with fecal microbiota transplant (FMT, transferring a healthy donor's whole gut-microbe community). This randomized phase 1b trial (early-stage, safety-and-signal) pits a defined, manufacturable 15-strain live biotherapeutic against same-donor FMT. Why it matters: a standardized strain cocktail would sidestep the variability and screening burden of whole-stool transplants. [Nature Medicine, 2026-06-02](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04442-2)
- **MET-inhibitor savolitinib shows activity in biomarker-selected stomach cancer (phase 2).** MET amplification (extra copies of the _MET_ gene, which drives tumor growth) marks a subset of gastric and gastroesophageal-junction cancers. Savolitinib blocks the resulting MET protein; this phase 2 (mid-stage efficacy) trial enrolled only MET-amplified patients. Why it matters: another data point for matching targeted drugs to a specific genetic driver rather than treating gastric cancer as one disease. [Nature Medicine, 2026-06-01](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04459-7)

## 📄 ML research — first arXiv batch

- **Hallucinations are linearly readable from a model's mid-layers — even after compression.** arXiv:2606.02628 (submitted 2026-06-03). A simple linear probe (a lightweight logistic readout on internal activations) applied to the middle layers cleanly separates truthful from fabricated outputs across Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B and Qwen2.5-7B, and the signal survives quantization (squeezing weights to low precision for cheap deployment). Why interesting: a near-free, built-in hallucination detector that still works on the compressed models people actually ship. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02628) `[preprint]`
- **More "thinking" can make reasoning models worse.** arXiv:2606.02835 (submitted 2026-06-03). Large reasoning models (LRMs) spend extra test-time compute writing out chain-of-thought (CoT — explicit step-by-step scratch work) before answering. This paper systematically measures _harmful overthinking_: cases where longer traces degrade accuracy. Why interesting: it pushes back on the "scale the reasoning length" reflex and argues for knowing when to stop. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02835) `[preprint]`
- **Giving robots a fixed-size memory instead of a ballooning KV-cache.** arXiv:2606.02775 (submitted 2026-06-03). The KV-cache (the running store of past tokens a transformer attends to) grows without bound over a long episode — tolerable in a datacenter that resets between short requests, ruinous for a robot running continuously. AURA proposes an action-gated memory that keeps GPU memory (VRAM) constant for embodied policies. Why interesting: a concrete path to robots that run for hours without memory blowing up. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02775) `[preprint]`
- **Faster diffusion-LLM decoding by deciding which tokens to commit together.** arXiv:2606.02955 (submitted 2026-06-03). Diffusion language models generate tokens in parallel rather than strictly left-to-right, but the bottleneck is judging which masked positions are safe to lock in simultaneously. Fast-dLLM++ refines that judgment (a "Fréchet profile" decoding criterion) on top of KV-caching to cut latency. Why interesting: parallel decoding is one of the few levers that can genuinely beat autoregressive generation speed. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02955) `[preprint]`
- **A skeptical look at what linear probes actually detect.** arXiv:2606.02907 (submitted 2026-06-03). Probing Qwen3-14B across logical, mathematical and commonsense benchmarks, the authors find linear probes track the _format_ of a task, not distinct internal "reasoning modes" — undercutting prior claims that models hold separable representations per reasoning type. Why interesting: a caution against over-interpreting probe results as evidence of structured internal cognition. [abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02907) `[preprint]`
