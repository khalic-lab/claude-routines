- **NextEra's $66.8B Dominion acquisition: the AI power infrastructure bet** — Bloomberg's May 28 analysis updates the strategic framing of NextEra Energy's $66.8B acquisition of Dominion Energy (announced May 18, SEC Form 425 filed). - **Colorado AI Act replaced: SB 26-189 eliminates high-risk compliance regime, shifts to transparency-only** — Governor Jared Polis signed SB 26-189 on May 14, 2026, repealing and replacing the 2024 Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205) with a substantially narrower framework. Gone are the duty of care, mandatory algorithmic impact assessments, and risk-management programme requirements that made the original law among the most burdensome in the United States. The new law applies only to "automated decision-making technologies" (ADMT) in consequential decisions (employment, housing, credit) and imposes four duties: (1) notify users when AI is involved; (2) disclose within 30 days if an adverse ADMT outcome occurs; (3) correct inaccurate personal data on request; (4) provide meaningful human review. Effective date: January 1, 2027. Enforcement caveat: Colorado's Attorney General has stated he will not enforce either the original or replacement law until rulemaking is complete. The repeal is significant because the 2024 Act had been used as a template in at least ten other state AI bills; its replacement removes the strongest existing state-level model in the US just as the DOJ AI Litigation Task Force is challenging state AI laws nationally.
