## 🌍 World politics — US morning

- **Iran resumes missile attacks on UAE; ceasefire effectively over:** Tehran fired four cruise missiles at the UAE — three shot down, one fell into the sea. An Iranian drone separately struck an oil facility in Fujairah, wounding three Indian nationals. The strikes ended the ~one-month US-Iran ceasefire and were the direct catalyst for Monday's equity sell-off and oil surge. ([NPR Iran war updates](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/04/nx-s1-5810508/iran-war-updates), 2026-05-04; [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/04/iran-war-trump-strait-of-hormuz.html), 2026-05-04) [via snippet]

- **"Project Freedom" — US guides ships through Strait of Hormuz:** Trump announced Sunday night a US military operation to guide neutral commercial shipping through the Strait. Assets committed: guided-missile destroyers, 100+ land- and sea-based aircraft, unmanned platforms, 15,000 service members. By Monday morning, two US-flagged merchant vessels had transited successfully. The operation is framed as "guidance," not formal naval escort — warships shadow rather than accompany commercial vessels. ([Time](https://time.com/article/2026/05/04/strait-hormuz-ships-guide-escort-ceasefire-us-iran-trump/), 2026-05-04; [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/u-s-says-2-merchant-ships-have-crossed-the-strait-of-hormuz-as-navy-helps-to-restore-shipping-traffic), 2026-05-04) [via snippet]

- **US-Iran direct fire exchange in the Strait:** US forces destroyed six Iranian small boats after Iran launched cruise missiles, drones, and fast-attack craft at US Navy ships and commercial vessels in the Strait. Iran claimed it struck a US warship; US Central Command denied the claim, stating no US Navy ships were hit. ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/04/strait-hormuz-us-merchant-ships-cross), 2026-05-04; [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/04/iran-war-trump-strait-of-hormuz.html), 2026-05-04) [via snippet]

- **Texas Senate District 4 special election:** Republican Brett Ligon, former Montgomery County DA, won Saturday's special election with ~75% of the vote, defeating Democrat Ron Angeletti. The seat was vacated when Senator Brandon Creighton was named chancellor of the Texas Tech University System. Ligon serves through January; a full-term rematch is set for November 2026. ([Texas Tribune](https://www.texastribune.org/2026/05/02/texas-senate-district-4-special-election/), 2026-05-02; [KSAT](https://www.ksat.com/news/texas/2026/05/03/republican-brett-ligon-former-montgomery-county-da-wins-special-election-for-open-texas-senate-seat/), 2026-05-03) [via snippet]
