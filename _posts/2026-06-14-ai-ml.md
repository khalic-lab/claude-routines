## 💼 Industry, funding, regulation

The one live thread today is **Chinese AI capital** — both a foundation-model lab and an
embodied-AI (robotics that perceives and physically acts in the world, as opposed to software-only
AI) startup moving on financing within the past week.

- **Moonshot AI is in talks to raise up to $2B at a ~$30B valuation — its third round in six
  months.** Per Bloomberg (8 June), the Beijing lab behind the **Kimi** chatbot has opened
  discussions for a new raise that would value it at roughly **$30 billion**, even as it is *still
  closing* a prior round led by **Meituan** (the Chinese food-delivery and local-services giant)
  that pegged it at **$20 billion**. If the new round lands at target, that's a **~7×** jump since
  December, when Moonshot was valued at just over **$4 billion** — an unusually steep re-rating
  driven by Kimi's commercial traction: annual recurring revenue (ARR — the run-rate of
  subscription income, annualised) reportedly **topped $200M in April**, roughly double the ~$100M
  at the start of March. _Why it matters:_ Moonshot is one of the Chinese labs (alongside DeepSeek,
  Qwen/Alibaba, GLM/Zhipu and MiniMax) driving the open-weight frontier this year, and the same
  week it shipped open weights for Kimi K2.7-Code it is reportedly raising at a valuation that
  rivals mid-tier US labs — a reminder that the open-weight push and the capital race are the same
  story, not two. The figures are reported, not confirmed by Moonshot, and the round is not closed.
  `[single-source]` `[disputed]` (valuations vary by outlet: $20B closing vs. $30B sought)
  ([Bloomberg, 2026-06-08](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-08/china-s-moonshot-ai-seeks-30-billion-value-in-new-funding-talks) · triangulation:
  [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/moonshot-ai-kimi-30-billion-valuation-china-ai-race)) `[via snippet]`

- **Humanoid-robot maker EngineAI files confidentially for a Hong Kong IPO.** Per Bloomberg
  (12 June), the Shenzhen embodied-AI startup **EngineAI** — founded 2023, builds general-purpose
  humanoid and quadruped robots with on-board perception-and-action AI for tasks like security
  patrols, retail service and industrial work — has filed confidential paperwork for a Hong Kong
  listing, working with **CICC** and **Citic Securities** on the deal. (A *confidential* filing
  lets a company start the regulatory review privately and only publish financials closer to the
  actual listing — increasingly the default for large tech IPOs.) The filing follows a **$200M
  Series B in April** that valued EngineAI at **$1.5 billion** (led by Henan CICC Huirong Fund and
  **Luxshare-ICT**, a major Apple-supply-chain electronics manufacturer), and a **12,000 m² factory**
  that opened 1 June claiming capacity for one humanoid every ~15 minutes. _Why it matters:_ China's
  humanoid-robot makers are racing to the public markets while the technology is still
  pre-revenue-at-scale, betting that manufacturing capacity plus "embodied AI" branding can pull
  forward demand — and Luxshare's involvement signals the consumer-electronics supply chain is
  positioning to mass-produce robot bodies the way it mass-produced phones. `[single-source]`
  ([Bloomberg, 2026-06-12](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-12/humanoid-robot-manufacturer-engineai-is-said-to-file-for-hong-kong-ipo) · triangulation:
