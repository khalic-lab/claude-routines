## 🔬 Science

- **Lithium extracted directly from brines without evaporation ponds.** Columbia Engineering researchers developed a temperature-sensitive solvent system that pulls lithium from low-grade underground brines in a single step, bypassing the multi-year evaporation ponds that currently dominate supply and drain local water tables. The method works on feedstocks that existing direct-lithium-extraction technologies cannot process economically. Timely given surging EV and grid-storage demand. [via snippet] — [_ScienceDaily_, 22 May 2026](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260522023132.htm)

- **Juno: Jovian megastorm lightning packs up to 100× Earth's punch.** Microwave-radiometer data from NASA's Juno spacecraft (83 orbits completed) reveal that some Jovian lightning bolts carry 100× — and possibly approaching 1 million× — the energy of terrestrial strikes. Storms build enormous charge over cloud tops exceeding 100 km altitude. Findings published in _Space_ magazine and covered by _ScienceDaily_ (21 May); prior radio-survey observations since 2016 underestimated peak energetics by an order of magnitude. [via snippet] — [_ScienceDaily_, 21 May 2026](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260520093756.htm) / [_Space_, 21 May 2026](https://www.space.com/astronomy/jupiter/lightning-on-jupiter-could-be-up-to-1-million-times-stronger-than-on-earth)

- **Irisin links exercise to neuroprotection in multiple sclerosis.** Wrann lab (Mass General Brigham / UKE Hamburg) shows that the muscle-derived hormone irisin protects spinal-cord neurons from inflammation-driven neurodegeneration in the EAE mouse model of MS. Peripheral irisin delivery reduces clinical symptoms and neuronal loss without altering central or peripheral immune responses; acts instead through a direct neuroprotective gene programme including preservation of synapses and mitochondrial activity. Authors flag progressive MS as a priority therapeutic target. [single-source] [via snippet] — [_Nature Metabolism_, 21 May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42255-026-01527-7)

- **AI disproves Erdős unit-distance conjecture — first major open math problem solved autonomously.** An OpenAI reasoning model produced a proof disproving the Erdős unit-distance conjecture (posed 1946): for infinitely many _n_, the maximum number of unit-distance pairs among _n_ points in the plane exceeds _n_^(1+δ) for some δ > 0. The key tool was not geometry but algebraic number theory — specifically infinite class field towers via the Golod-Shafarevich theorem, a 1960s result. The proof was independently verified by Noga Alon (Princeton), Melanie Wood, and Thomas Bloom (maintainer of the Erdős Problems database). [via snippet] — [_Scientific American_, May 2026](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/ai-just-solved-an-80-year-old-erdos-problem-and-mathematicians-are-amazed/) / [_TechCrunch_, 20 May 2026](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/openai-claims-it-solved-an-80-year-old-math-problem-for-real-this-time/)

- **JWST maps rocky exoplanet surface for the first time: dark basalt, no air.** Zieba, Kreidberg et al. (Harvard-Smithsonian / MPIA) used JWST's MIRI instrument to obtain a 5–12 µm thermal emission spectrum of LHS 3844 b — a super-Earth 48.5 ly away with an 11-hour orbit. Best-fit surface is dark, low-silica rock (basalt or olivine-rich) shaped by space weathering. Data rule out a substantial atmosphere: CO₂ < 100 mbar (5σ) and SO₂ < 10 µbar (3σ), eliminating widespread active volcanism. First time surface geology — not just the atmosphere — of a rocky exoplanet has been directly studied. [via snippet] — [_Nature Astronomy_, May 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41550-026-02860-3) / [preprint arXiv:2605.00100](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00100)
