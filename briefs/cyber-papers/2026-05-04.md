- **arXiv:2605.00347 (submitted May 1, 2026) — Odysseus: Scaling VLMs to 100+ Turn Decision-Making in Games via Reinforcement Learning** `[preprint]` — Princeton and collaborators train a vision-language model to play Super Mario Land via RL, requiring 100+ turns of coordinated perception, reasoning, and action — one of the longest-horizon RL setups applied to a pretrained VLM. Core finding: a **PPO variant with a lightweight turn-level critic** substantially outperforms critic-free alternatives (GRPO, Reinforce++) in both training stability and sample efficiency. Pretrained VLM priors reduce the need for manual action engineering compared to classical deep RL. Results: ≥3× average game progress vs. frontier models; cross-level and cross-game generalization maintained without degrading general-domain capabilities. Relevant for: long-horizon agentic RL, stable multi-turn value estimation, and VLM-as-policy training. [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.00347](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00347)

- **arXiv:2605.00425 (submitted May 1, 2026) — AEM: Adaptive Entropy Modulation for Multi-Turn Agentic Reinforcement Learning** `[preprint]` — Multi-turn agentic RL with sparse, outcome-only rewards makes credit assignment at individual steps intractable; existing fixes (process reward models, auxiliary self-supervised signals) add supervision complexity and generalize poorly. AEM is a **supervision-free** method that adaptively modulates entropy dynamics during RL training to achieve a better exploration–exploitation trade-off without adding any extra models or datasets. The adaptive entropy signal effectively serves as a lightweight dense signal inferred directly from training dynamics. Relevant for: agentic tool-use RL, web-agent and code-agent training, and any setting where intermediate-step supervision is expensive. [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.00425](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00425)

- **arXiv:2605.00365 (submitted May 1, 2026) — Uniform-Correct Policy Optimization: Breaking RLVR's Indifference to Diversity** `[preprint]` — RLVR (RL with Verifiable Rewards) improves Pass@1 on reasoning benchmarks but causes **diversity collapse**: Pass@K degrades because standard RLVR objectives do not distinguish between concentrating all probability mass on one correct solution vs. distributing it across many. This paper formalises RLVR's "indifference to diversity" and proposes Uniform-Correct Policy Optimization (UCPO), which reformulates the objective to incentivize a uniform distribution over the full set of correct outputs. Relevant for: production systems where sample diversity matters (e.g., code generation, mathematical reasoning with multiple valid proofs), test-time scaling strategies, and coverage-oriented evaluation. [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.00365](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00365)

- **arXiv:2605.00380 (submitted May 1, 2026) — ResRL: Boosting LLM Reasoning via Negative Sample Projection Residual Reinforcement Learning** `[preprint]` — Complementary to diversity-collapse fixes, ResRL adds a **negative sample projection** (NSP) residual term to the RLVR objective. Where standard RLVR only pulls the policy toward successful reasoning chains, NSP explicitly pushes it away from failed reasoning paths using gradient residuals computed from negative samples — no additional models, datasets, or labeling required. This avoids the asymmetric pressure that causes mode collapse while improving reasoning diversity and coverage. Evaluated on math and code tasks. Relevant for: RLVR training recipes, reasoning model post-training, and diversity in multi-step chain-of-thought generation. [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.00380](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00380)

- **arXiv:2605.00654 (submitted May 1, 2026) — Reinforcement Learning with Markov Risk Measures and Multipattern Risk Approximation** `[preprint]` — Introduces a class of Markov coherent risk measures called "mini-batch measures" and defines **multipattern risk-averse RL problems** that generalize the standard linear (expectation-based) formulation. Derives tractable policy-optimization objectives for this broader class and provides convergence guarantees. Relevant for: safety-constrained RL agents, financial portfolio/operations research applications where tail-risk control matters, and risk-sensitive robotics. Distinct from the dominant expected-reward paradigm in production RL. [via snippet] — [arXiv:2605.00654](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00654)
